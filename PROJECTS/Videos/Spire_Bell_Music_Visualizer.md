# OVERVIEW
An audio music video / visualizer showcasing "Spire Bell", The Spireknell (Lithe)'s boss theme and first true boss encounter in Firmata Frontier. The video itself takes heavy inspiration from Deltarune's Roaring Knight boss fight in Chapter 3, and The Worldsmith from the fanmade Deltarune: Chapter Rewritten— specifically, its own audio music video, "Hell Hammer".
However, contrary to the "Hell Hammer" music video, "Spire Bell" will feature a small animated cutscene in the very beginning that does *not* feature Deltarune-style sprites. Alongside that, it also swaps between a frame-by-frame animated line-boiled artwork of Lithe / The Spireknell with an audio visualizer Spire in the background, and an actual faked-gameplay Deltarune-styled battle scene of some conceptual attacks an actual "Deltarune-Style Lithe/Spireknell Bossfight" would heave, featuring a unique soul mode (alongside replacing the soul with a skribble), a unique health system, and other unique attacks/mechanics.

# Deltarune-Style Spireknell Concept
Sections of the Idle Animation + Music Visualizer have a Deltarune-inspired battle animation spliced in between them at certain times.
Replace Deltarune's Red Heart Soul with a Pale-Green Skribble Flame.
Replace Green bounding box with sky blue bounding box (have it animate coming from the sky rather than rotating in and out)
No "playable character" is shown (for now).

## "Soul Gimmick"
### ["Signature Soul"] (Candidate 1) (Easier but boring)
During the opening Time Signature Roar, the scribble would be on-screen. For each sound cue, summon 1 new "arrow", initially starting above, then add one below. On the 3rd cue, add a 3rd arrow but make them face diagonally top left, diagonally top right, and bottom. And on the fourth cue, make all arrows face up, down, left, right.
In "Signature Soul" mode, you can ONLY move in the directions the arrows point at. If there's no arrow facing diagonally, you can't move diagonally.
Midway through the track, The Spireknell will use Time Signature Roar again, swapping it to a different 4/4 arrangement where the player can only move diagonally now.

### ["Signature Soul"] (Candidate 2) (Harder but interesting)
During the opening Time Signature Roar, the scribble would be on-screen. For each sound cue, slowly extend a new bar in the bottom of the screen. On the 5th cue that finalizes the bar, this becomes the "Sheet Bar".
During the track, "notes" would move in from the side towards the center of the bar. They will reach the center in-sync to the track's time signature. When pressing the "interact" button (gameplay-wise), release a pulse around the Skribble, which is capable of destroying bullet patterns. This means the player can "fight back" against The Spireknell, but only "on beat".

## ["Firmata Frontier Health Gimmick"] (Memoriam Tabs)
When getting hit, rather than health being depleted, the game animation freezes for a moment. The screen darkens and 4 pale green stars (resonance) show above the skribble. After a short animation of one star being slashed and destroyed, the game resumes, with particles flowing to Lithe. These form "Memoriam Tabs" on Cara Bell / Lithe, acting like a mana system for more attacks.
Rather than Deltarune's HP-based health system, this health system is "heart/hits-focused". You can only take 4 hits before being defeated.
And, yes, all music/rhythm-based bullets/elements (like Swing Rhythm's notes) still move during stopped time. A second of invulnerability is given at the very least.

## Attack / Bullet pattern ideas
### [Swing Rhythm]
Lithe flings Cara Bell around like a magician casting a spell, or a flail wielder... ok yeah no this action is more magician-like.
Each fling creates bullet patterns shaped like piano roll notes which are synced to the music. They fly from the right to the left towards the player, with them hitting the left side of the bounding box "on sync" to the music.
(Animation Idea: Animate the notes BACKWARD, coming from the left side of the box on beat, the animating BACK from Cara Bell)

### [Time Signature Roar]
#### (Candidate 1)
A simple wave of bullet patterns coming from Lithe that you need to dodge while the new time signature gets set (1/4 meaning you can only move up. Then it becomes 2/4, only up and down movement. Then 3/4, where it's an inverse of the up-left and up-right foreshadowed earlier, now being down-left down-right and up. Then back to 4/4, but only diagonals now rather than straight up-down-left-right quadratic)
#### (Candidate 2)
Several "un-dodgable" waves from Lithe. You NEED to parry these on-beat to destroy a hole for yourself to move through.

### [Bell Curve]
Lithe spins around, teleports around the scene, and tossing Cara Bell back and forth across the bounding box. On the last teleport, Lithe tosses Cara Bell down, creating a similar burst of particles as seen in the "HER Judge" attack.

### [HER Judge]
Lithe throws and reels-back Cara Bell in and out the bounding box, creating sound waves on each "throw apex", similar to Deltarune Chapter 1's Chaos King flail attack where he tosses his tail at the soul.
However, on the last throw, The Spireknell lobs Cara Bell into the air in an arch, contrary to the previous linear throws. Once it lands, it delays for half a second before releasing a burst of projectiles in a radius, then realing Cara Bell back in. This attack directly mirrors and references an attack from Hollow Knight: Silksong's  "Last Judge" boss.
