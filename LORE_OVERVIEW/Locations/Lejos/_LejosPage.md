## Idea
Currently suffering the most from "The Spirepocalypse" as its entire central continents were enveloped by "Her Firmata", cutting off An-Tri'Fhiana and all of the major natural resources in the untapped lands away from the modernizing nations of this Exspanse. Aside from that, they were historically known for being able to tame and breed caradels into pets and companions, similar to pokemon.

## Overview
> A land of wildlife and science, the Lejos Exspanse is a vast and varied continental archipelago situated the furthest distance north east from any other known exspanse (this isolation is intended). While carrying a varried and cultured history with Fracturing Phenomena, Caradel training, and Avalonian Expansionism, this all got burried and swept away following The Spirepocalypse— when the Living Piece of Morgale de Metso attempted to achieve the Epicenter of Fiction, using the power to create her own firmata within the center of the exspanse.
> Today, Lejos is divided into the outer dome and inner dome.

## The Inner Dome, "HER Firmata".
> The Inner dome is where "The Continent" is situated in and where Firmata Frontier, and Frontline Firmata takes place in.
> It is a wild and untamed wilderness due to the Avalonian Outpost 3, An-Tri'Fhiana, taking control over the region. Any expansionism efforts by the AoS and any "Outer Dome" nation (known simply as Lejeseh)

## The Outer Dome, "THEIR Firmata"
> Filled with people who see the "inner dome" daily and constantly build resentment over the "domers" who reside within, unknowing to the fact that they themselves are within a Firmata as well, the Outer Dome is where the rest of Lejos lives almost uninterupted by The Spirepocalypse, outside of losing access to the central wilds of their Exspanse due to HER Firmata.

### Cordistas
> People, or equipment used, in "tuning" caradels, which is how they tame and tweak the purpose/use of a caradel to match what they're intending for. The process is similar to tuning an instrument, where they lightly hammer or dampen the vibration of a caradel while speaking into it through a glass (or clay) cup pressed onto the caradel. Specific words and terms get amplified when hammered, while dampening nullifies them.
