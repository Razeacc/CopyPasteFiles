## Overview
> A vast and varied continental archipelago, the **Lejos Exspanse** is situated at the furthest possible northeast distance from any other known Exspanse. This extreme geographic isolation is entirely intentional. 
> 
> Lejos boasts a rich, cultured history shaped by Fracturing Phenomena, Caradel training, and Avalonian Expansionism. However, this history was entirely buried and swept away during **The Spirepocalypse**—the cataclysmic event where the Living Piece of Morgale de Metso attempted to achieve the Epicenter of Fiction. She weaponized this power to manifest her own *firmata* right at the heart of the Exspanse.
> 
> Today, Lejos is permanently severed into two distinct realities: the **Inner Dome** and the **Outer Dome**.

---

## The Inner Dome: "HER Firmata"
The Inner Dome houses "The Continent," serving as the primary setting for the *Firmata Frontier* and *Frontline Firmata*. 

*   **The Wilds**: It is an untamed, perilous wilderness ruled by the Avalonian Outpost 3, **An-Tri'Fhiana**. Housed within a massive underground network, these Avalonians have completely thwarted all expansion efforts by the AoS and the Outer Dome nations.
*   **Geography**: The landmass consists of a southwestern and a northeastern continent, with a single Spire Island located near the center. The northeastern continent acts as the true logical epicenter of "HER Firmata," a geographical fact only recognizable from a bird's-eye view. 
*   **Climate Anomalies**: The climate reverses standard physics; the landscape is cold and barren at its base, but heats up drastically the higher you climb and the closer you get to the edges of the Firmata boundary, akin to getting closer or further from a star.
*   **The Memory Curse**: During The Spirepocalypse, the Living Piece of Morgale de Metso stripped away all memories existing within the boundary. Anyone carrying memories who enters HER Firmata without *Beyonder protection* will have their mind instantly wiped by the domain. Intruders who somehow bypass this curse are hunted down by **Firmata Paladin units** or **The Spireknell (Lithe)**.
*   **Time Dilation**: Time flows exponentially faster inside the Inner Dome. While only roughly 20 years have passed in the outside world since the Spirepocalypse, **hundreds or thousands of years** have elapsed inside. Morgale's Living Piece intended this time dilation to act as a "second chance" for Avalon to rebuild from its ashes. Knowing that Lithe retained his memories, she placed her faith in him to forge an Avalon strong enough to withstand the forces of the outside world.
*   **Ecology**: Taking inspiration from Ice Age-era flora and fauna, the wildlife of The Continent consists of creatures from the epoch just after the dinosaurs but right before modern wildlife. Inhabitants can encounter woolly mammoths, saber-toothed tigers, and even dodo birds. 
*   **Inner Caradel Synthesis**: Due to rigorous testing and experiments by the An-Tri'Fhianan Avalonians, Caradels here are only artificially created within isolated Fracturing Zones inside their compounds. This stands in stark contrast to the naturally-born Caradels harvested from the Fracturing Sea by the rest of the Lejoseh in the Outer Dome.

## The Outer Dome, "THEIR Firmata"
The Outer Dome is home to the remaining nations of Lejos, living in the shadow of the barrier. Its citizens look at the Inner Dome daily, entirely unaware that they themselves are trapped inside a massive *firmata* as well.

*   **The Geopolitical Divide**: The nations here are heavily fractured by a fierce ideological split regarding the Inner Dome population (whom they call the **"Lejeseh"**).
    *   *The Hostile*: Those who believe the inner inhabitants are evil archkin destined to end the world (coining the derogatory slur **"Domers"**).
    *   *The Diplomatic*: Those who advocate for negotiation, driven either by fear of the dome's power or mercy for its captives.
    *   *The Explorers*: Those driven by curiosity to see if life still exists inside, with some brave (or foolish) souls attempting to breach HER Firmata.
*   **The Retro-Tech Society**: Safe from the memory wipe, the Outer Dome safely preserved their history. They have evolved into a relatively modern society mirroring real-world **late 1940s to early 1980s cultures**. 
*   **The Caradel Net**: Lacking traditional modern infrastructure, they have built a **pseudo-internet** entirely powered by trained Caradels. By exploiting *The Fracturing's* innate psychic connection linking all Caradels, the Outer Dome organizes communication, media, and data sharing on a massive, global scale.

## Terminologies

### Lejeseh 
*   **Pronunciation**: *leh-HEH-seh*
*   **Definition**: The demonym for the people of Lejos. In the Outer Dome, this term is specifically used to refer to themselves, helping spearate them from the Inner Dome denizens through a derogatory slur.

### Domer
*   **Pronunciation**: *dow-mer*
*   **Definition**: A derogatory slur used by Outer Dome citizens to refer to those within the Inner Dome. Because the Outer Dome citizens are entirely unaware that they are also trapped inside a Firmata, the term is highly ironic. By the time characters from *Firmata Frontier* learn the word and manage to escape HER Firmata, they will already know the truth about THEIR Firmata. Consequently, the term ceases to be an insult and instead serves as tragic evidence of the grand lie keeping the Outer Dome in the dark.

### Cordistas
*   **Definition**: The specialized acoustic experts—or the dedicated vibrational tools—used to "tune" and tame Caradels. 
*   **The Process**: Similar to tuning an instrument, a Cordista presses a specialized glass or clay cup against a Caradel's vibrational form. By speaking specific commands into the cup while lightly hammering or dampening the creature's vibrations, they alter its behavior. Amplifying specific spoken words enhances certain traits, while physical dampening nullifies unwanted behaviors, turning mindless soul constructs into loyal companions, tools, or weapons. 
*   **Advanced Conditioning**: With sufficient training and specialized equipment (known as *aparti*), a Cordista can physically modify and upgrade a Caradel's structure. Artificial breeding is also possible, though this process is heavily gatekept by clandestine specialists due to its immense complexity and profound ethical controversies. Culturally, it is widely considered morally superior to adopt an existing Caradel rather than fabricate a new one.


### Lejoseh Caradels
*   **Definition**: A unique strain of Caradels exclusive to the region. Unlike standard Caradels, which lack true sentience and carry fractured memories, Lejoseh Caradels are infused with the lingering, miasmic sentience of **The First Archgod Dragon**, who was slain long ago. 
*   **The Archose Effect**: The dragon's *miasmic archose* has seeped deeply into the spirite leylines of Lejos. When mindless Caradels pass through these leylines to "fabricate" into reality, they are granted a sliver of innate sentience—just enough for reasoning, basic training, and even structural evolution. 
*   **Societal Roles**: These entities are seamlessly integrated into retro-tech society as personal phones, digital assistants, or household appliances like televisions, computers, microwaves, and refrigerators (utilizing their internal vibrations to instantly heat or cool objects).
*   **The Psychology of Purpose**: Because they exist in a permanently fragmented, partially "lobotomized" mental state, they feel no malice or sorrow regarding their servitude. In fact, being assigned a fixed purpose grounds their fragile consciousness in reality. "Freed" Caradels lacking an owner often become profoundly lost and violently unstable, as they lack the processing capacity or depth of sentience to handle absolute freedom. Remarkably, Lejoseh Caradels can cross the borders of Lejos at any time while perfectly retaining this baseline sentience.

## History

### Early Lejos: The First Archgod Dragon
> Discovering a cluster of isolated islands far from the rest of civilization, an unnamed Archgod Dragon chose these lands as their sanctuary. The dragon punctured the earth to raise Spires from the spirite leylines below, pulling new continents up from the seabed and birthing their own sovereign territory. 
> 
> Desiring absolute isolation from other archkin, this gentle and quiet Archgod rarely received visitors from their own ilk, choosing to coexist exclusively with the humans they had created. Even then, the dragon remained a distant protector, constantly circling the Exspanse to guard its borders while rarely interacting directly with the populace—wishing only to watch over them from afar.

### The First Caradel Swarm & The Silent Assassination
> The peace was shattered during a routine patrol when the first wave of Caradels unexpectedly emerged from the seas of Lejos. The mindless constructs swarmed the populated islands, relentlessly attacking both the human citizens and the Archgod Dragon. 
> 
> This unprecedented crisis triggered a call to arms across The Infinite Fields, prompting archkin from all over the world to march toward Lejos in defense of their peer. However, the closest available deity was **Morgale de Metso**. 
> 
> Arriving ahead of the others, the entity publicly known as the "weakest archgod" was secretly contacted by **Nul Detta**—the ancient enemy of archkin society and the cosmic source of the Caradels. Acting as Nul Detta's sleeper agent, Morgale was given her first true mission: assassinate the Archgod Dragon before the other archkin could arrive. 
> 
> Left with no alternative, Morgale took advantage of the absolute chaos as the Caradels swarmed the dragon. In a single, devastating movement, she unveiled her hidden, true strength, striking down the nameless Archgod with a solitary blow from her hammer. As the dragon fell, its decaying life force began seeping into the earth, transforming into a **Miasmic Archose**. This corruption was Nul Detta's true objective—planting the exact cosmic seed required to synthesize Lejoseh Caradels in the future. 
> 
> When the remaining archkin forces finally arrived, Morgale effortlessly hid her treachery, assisting them in purging the remaining Caradel swarm from Lejos.

### Outpost 3: The Legacy of Betrayal
> When the Avalonian Exodus arrived to assist in rebuilding the devastated Exspanse, the gathered archkin debriefed on the tragedy, realizing the nameless dragon had perished. It was the first true death of an Archgod since the conclusion of The Creation War. 
> 
> While Khueen was completely devastated by the loss, Morgale maintained her silent deception. After the other Archgods eventually retreated back to their respective homelands, Morgale chose to remain in the Exspanse. 
> 
> Under the guise of a protector, she established **Outpost 3** and the subterranean network of **An-Tri'Fhiana**. This position allowed her to secretly reap the benefits of her ancient assassination, kickstarting the forbidden research into the newly emerging, sentient Lejoseh Caradels.
