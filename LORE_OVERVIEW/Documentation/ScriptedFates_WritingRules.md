# Overview
This acts as a simple guide not meant for any audiences to see, unless specifically given behind-the-scenes access.
These writing rules are similar to the "Game Design Rules" implemented in Minecraft by Mojang, steps and precautions to take in ensuring Scripted Fates maintains its specific vibe no matter how much it expands.

## Scripted Fates' Core Theme
While it does carry themes of connection/separation (Fracturing Seperatist frequencies vs Archkin unifying archose hiveminds / Epicenters), tackles deep topics like whether or not archkin should exist, etc.
At the core of these stories, Scripted Fates is a "**Celebration of Fiction and a Love letter to Creativity**".
Its main goal is to redefine the meaning of fiction, help people rediscover why fiction is so important, and what fiction means to us as "real" people.

## Writing Rules

###     1. Magic is always Science
Even if it's never explained, magic should always have a certain level of consistency. Applying scientific applications to magic should always be plausible and reasonable. Just like how fire and electricity can appear "magical"  to an ancient person, magic should always be grounded in consistent rules outside of its mystique; even if these rules exist merely behind the scenes.

###     2. Always Ambiguous Afterlife
The Afterlife should be treated as openly as possible. Never alude to what the afterlife in Scripted Fates looks like.
**The Null Crossing is not considered an afterlife**, as it's Nul Detta whisking away souls from ceasing to exist.
_Ceasing to exist_, or _crossing over into Entropy / Endless Entropy_ is also **not an afterlife**, as it is _complete annihilation_.
This leaves afterlife ideas completely open for anyone to theorize on.

###     3. Never Add Lore to The 4th Beyond's World
Our world IS The 4th Beyond's World, as _WE ARE The 4th Beyond_. If you were to add lore to our world, it'd have to be something miniscule in the distant past. It should be impossible to validate/disprove (IE: Ulhysse's origins). Or, it should be something you ACTUALLY do in the real world.
<small>IE: how the Scripted Fates Fandom server  will eventually be overrun by Nul Detta, explaining why we'd transition over into something like wiki.gg at some point.</small>
This rule also extends to _a ban on writing lore on OUR OWN mythologies / cosmologies_, as that would imply they're fictional, reducing what should be a reality layer above us, to something beneath us instead.

###     4. Treat Fiction as Sentient
If Scripted Fates was a Pixar movie, it'd be "What if Fictional Characters had feelings". This is akin to a Fiction version of Toy Story (IE: Toys Being Alive is to Fictional Characters Being Alive). 
The feeling Scripted Fates should aim for is "man, I wonder what things would be like if these guys were actually out there somewhere...". And due to how the cosmology of Scripted Fates work, this should be a completely valid thought/feeling to have. The stories should feel like they exist **beyond** the page (hence why The 4th Beyond is called such in the first place)

###     5. Never explain certain characters/mechanics
*   Why The Faith -> Ascension system works
*   Whatever the fuck The Equilibrium Arbiter, Ultimata Spectata (including all connected ideas to him), Simulette, and Syde Aion are
These ideas should remain whimsical and mysterious, to never be given explanation. These are the "distant blurry mountains" of Scripted Fates' lore.

###     6. The Toyette should be unpredictable
The Toyette is sentient due to Tool's control in the future. Therefore, it should be smart and hard to predict consistently.

###     7. Never write passed the mainline Scripted Fates games
Anything beyond Tool's usage of The Epicenter of Fiction to commence The Great Crunch, and the ending he achieves from this, should be cut off completely. A vote will be those for all Scripted Fates fans at the end of the series to either end of continue the story.
Only write further beyond the planned ending if fans want to continue.

###     8. Always make new stories/projects digestible to first-time fans
The fun with Scripted Fates is that you can start with Firmata Frontier, Beyond Eternity, Farewell Avalon, Project: FOURTH MARSHAL, or even Hinokami Caretaker. And all relevant ideas in those stories are always explained and introduced in their respective stories as if it was your first time learning about them.
Only ideas not critical to the story can be left unexplained, to be clarified further in other projects. Viewers/Players should be able to experience any Scripted Fates project in any order (with the exception of any sequels, of course. Like Firmata Frontier to Frontline Firmata, or Farewell Avalon to Trek to Avalon), and still be able to make sense of the stories they encompass.
Do NOT do interconnected stories like Marvel, where you almost HAVE to watch everything else to make sense of future/current stories. Treat Scripted Fates projects similar to Jojo's Bizarre Adventure, where all relevant ideas are re-explained or re-introduced in the beginning of each story/project.

###     9. The Fourth Beyond's Decrees on Archkin Society MUST get more complex over time
"Over Time" refers to the in-story chronology. The initial and early decrees must be simple (IE: Make Archkin, get annuled. Break character, get annuled. Etc.). But over time, later on in the story, they must get more and more complicated. (IE: Fail to acquire the Epicenter of Fiction, ~~get annuled~~ turn into a Failed Epicenter. Make a timeliner do quantum suicide, ~~get annuled~~ get **stalked** more).

###     10. Mantain the 4 Ontological Classifications
Ensuring the 4 Ontologies remain consistent is one of the most important rules in insuring Scripted Fates feels like a cohesive overarching setting. It is paramount that, whenever ontologies are relevant, these ideas should remain consistent:
*   **Nul**: <u>1st Beyond</u>, represents story creators, free will among fiction, and those wishing to "break" the story's script. Ironically, although it's meant to be Nul Detta, members of The Fourth Beyond constantly tap into this Ontological class when making Alternate Universe (AU) stories, fanfictions, headcanons, etc. This Beyond represents the **backstage**.
*   **Arch**: <u>2nd Beyond</u>, are characters with innate magical/fantastical abilities, representing left-stage actors and performers in less realistic stories and settings.
*   **Mortal**: <u>3rd Beyond</u>, are characters without innate magic, representing right-stage actors and performers for stories of the more grounded settings/context.
*   **Fourth**: <u>4th Beyond</u>, represents the audience, other creators who make their own works, and "story moderators" (IE: People who work with the author to keep a story in-check, like editors or server staff).

###     11. Audience/Author Role Clarifications
For most roleplayers/players, they use a skribble which controls fate around a character. It's not entirely sure if the skribble also controls the character, or if they have free will to merely be "acting on" the skribble's fate manipulation.
As for most authors, they use a Worldbuilder through Sandatta Ulhysse to create their settings.
However, "That Fourth Marshal" (which is the in-lore name used for Razeacc, aka Me, which is akin to how Toby Fox has an "in-lore" character as the "Annoying Dog"), is different, in a way where they're an active traitor to the court. As Scripted Fates' writer themself, Razeacc works WITH Null Detta to let them both share control over vicars and other entities that normally would be outside of The Court's control. This would explain how characters like Memetta, Familiar Familiar, Lithe, etc. can still be roleplayed by Razeacc/Rayzie despite them canonically NOT having skribbles.

## Game Design Rules

###     1. Gameplay Mechanics must ALWAYS be connected to the world/story
If a gameplay mechanic does not make sense in the world, breaks immersion, and makes players say "Let's not think about this TOO hard it's just a game after all", make SURE there is an actual explanation for it down the line.
For instance, Firmata Frontier's rolling system was made by Morgale's Living Piece in order to "Make the Roleplayers have more fun within Her Firmata" alongside seeing if "a certain level of chaos can help the Roleplayers break the pre-assigned fates written by The Fourth Beyond AND Nul Detta". The Rolling System is essentially an experiment on fate by Morgale's Living Piece.
If a mechanic is ever lazily added in without a story explanation, it is of utmost importance that it gets an explanation eventually.

###     2. Modi, skills, abiliies, etc. should have more than JUST combat applications
Whether through a character's own creativity or flexible limitations, characters should not feel like their skills/abilites have PURELY combat applications/uses... that is, unless it perfectly fits their character.
A good example is Ayumu, who can travel beyond light speed to rewind time and change fate. Not only can she use it in combat be pre-loading damage onto enemies, she can also use it to relay instuctions and messages to the present from the future.

###     3. Beyond Light Speed Rewrites Fate
Beyond Light Speed Travel works differently in this story's logic. While teleportation is still possible through space manipulation and directly tapping into the world coordinates of objects, going beyond light speed through actual speed and acceleration creates a whole new concept entirely.
After light speed, all added speed spills rolls over into negative speed, akin to variables reaching the 32-bit integer limit. Once this is achieved, you're capable of seeing time itself flow behind you as you yourself also move backward in time. Although almost impossibly-hard to achieve, this is one of the safest ways to "traverse time" outside of timelines in the Scripted Fates series.
However, actually manipulating time this way is its own ability altogether. If you can somehow make new/different moves while rewinding, not only do you overextend your own capabilities, but it also subjects your de-syncing bodyparts to tiny swarms of temporal bubble detonations, which can quickly spiral into your whole body getting temporally-detonated if you can't deal with the pain.
> Note: This still differs from Ayumu's Modus, which explicitely states her armor allows her to move within this rewinding time and CHANGE/Preload fate by protecting her from the temporal bubble detonations.

###     4. Creativity MUST be rewarded
Whether through a character who would realistically come up with a game-breaking way to use a system, or a player discovering a "fun way" to use a mechanic or bug, you should NEVER punish creativity and ALWAYS work around it.
A character who creatively broke the system, let them keep the "break" even if The Court of the Fourth eventually patches it out. Let them be powerful.
A player who discovers new emergent gameplay, try your best to adjust the game design around it, especially if it genuinely is fun, EVEN if it's more fun than the original idea. Just try your best to avoid breaking the lore while doing so. Or, if possbile, think of a way on how to bend the lore.
A good example of this second idea is Archie from Farewell Avalon originally having very "weird" dash mechanics. They could dash a second and third time if done at certain frame-specific times after the first dash, even during cooldown. Rather than patching this out, it was refined, polished, and advertised as "High-Intensity Dashing". 

## Music Rules

###     1. Avoid ScriFa-Specific Lyrics
When writing lyrics, try your best to avoid lyrics that only make sense to ScriFa fans. Avoid name-dropping characters, phenomena, concepts, etc. as much as possible.
Instead, use ambiguous wording, metaphorical phrasing, and terms that most people can at least somewhat understand the idea and meaning of, but not fully grasp.
So words like "Fracturing" to directly refer to The Fracturing is fine. But "NulGraven" should instead be something like "Vermillion" or "Red Spread" when used in lyrics.
