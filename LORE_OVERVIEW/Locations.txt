-#####		"The Continent"
{{ScriFa_Location|title1="The Continent"|image1=TheContinent FIRMATA.png|habitability=Decent|climate_type=High-Contrasting Regions|notable_resources=[[Spirite]]|anomaly=[["The Spire"]]|current_factions=[[The Stone Society]]|historical_factions=[[The Avalonian Exodus]]|settlement_status=Sparsely Populated|threat_level=Low-High|resource_density=Clusters}}

The main setting of [[Firmata Frontier]], it is a large barely-mapped continent of sweltering sandy mountains and cool temperate forested fields. Off the coast of this land, far off into the [[Fracturing Sea]], an island hosting [["The Spire"]], alongside the petrified growths that surround it, can be seen at all times of day.

Although there is a proper name for this location, it is currently unknown and lost to the memory wipe. Later on, perhaps survivors of [[The Spirepocalypse]] may found a new name to these lands for themselves.

Several settlements can be found on "The Continent", with all inhabited ones being in allegiance with [[The Stone Society]].

= Environment Overview =

== The Sky ==
Contrary to what most [["Otherworlders"]] may expect, there is '''no sun nor moon''' in The Continent's atmosplane. Despite that, the sky itself seems to brighten and dim on a regular even 12 hour basis. Although some days or nights may be shorter or longer at times, for the most part, the sky seems to function perfectly fine without the two most commonly-expected celestial bodies.
However, at night, countless stars may dart the nightsky. Upon closer inspection, to some's dismay, even these stars may appear quite bizarre. They shift and change positon constantly, blinkin in and out of place. And, on rare occasions, all these stars blink simultaniously, catching any star watches potentially off-guard.

== The Winds ==
Most breezes seem to blow towards the direction of [["The Spire"]], or rather, a certain direction beyond deep across the [["The Continent"#Fracturing Sea|Fracturing Sea]]. It is unknown what's causing such air currents yet.

== Fauna ==
Teaming with strange and absurdly familiar but almost unknown creatures to [[you]], The Continent's fauna consists of mostly extinct creatures from [[You|your]] world. Ranging from the well-known Dodo bird and Woolly Mammoths, to the sea being haunted by a Fissuring Megalodon.

Although, it seems most creatures around "The Continent" are adapted to colder environments, adding emphasis to locations like the [["The Continent"#The Sweltertops|Sweltertops]], [["The Continent"#The “Western Savannah”|Western Savannah]], and [["The Continent"#Desert Edges|Desert Edges]] severely lacking in biodiversity. They seemingly follow a trend of being creatures typically from an "Ice Age" era, meaning there are '''no massive insects''' nor '''large reptilian dinosaurs'''.

== Flora ==
Just like the creatures, most plants seem to be species of flora that have ceased to exist from your world. Pushed to extinction there, they were given a new life here. However, digging deep into the roots of these plants, the bases of all older trees seem to be particularly hardened in almost unnatural layers.

= Settlements =
== San Baciato ==
{{ScriFa Location|title1=San Baciato|habitability=Pristine|climate_type=Beach-side Hills|notable_resources=|current_factions=[[The Stone Society]]|historical_factions=???|settlement_status=Dwindling Habitation|threat_level=Low|resource_density=|anomaly=???|image1=SanBaciato OnMap.jpg}}
The '''oldest settlement''' on the continent.<br />A quiet aged coastal town built from makeshift seaweed plaster and thatched roofs, known for its serene beaches, washed up coral bits, beachside relaxation, sea shells, and its much older but gentler population.

San Baciato serves as a rest stop for travellers reckless enough to brave [["The Continent"#The Sweltertops|The Sweltertops]], while also acting as the most peaceful and quietest town among [[The Stone Society]]. With such a pristine and calm beach, [[The Last Midwife]] can be found here strolling the sands every midnight.

A small tree-house on the settlement’s edge serves as the home of [[Professor Besado]].

* Purpose: '''Recreational Saltwater Fishing'''
* Established: '''19 Years Ago'''
* Population: '''9-12 People'''
* Story Character Inhabitants:
** [[Professor Besado]]
** [[The Last Midwife]]

== Erstburg ==
{{ScriFa Location|title1=Erstburg|habitability=Moderate|climate_type=Savannah - Boreal|notable_resources=Mineral-laced Fish|current_factions=[[The Stone Society]]|historical_factions=|settlement_status=Average Habitation|threat_level=Medium|resource_density=|anomaly=|image1=Erstburg OnMap.jpg}}

A riverside fishing town positioned beside the edge of the [["The Continent"#Dregwoods|Dregwoods]].<br />Home to many middle-aged settlers and Otherworlders seeking the balance of a stable work life while not having to deal with the absolute bustle of a borderline stone-age city.  

Erstburg maintains a strong '''fishing''' and '''hunting''' culture while acting as the Stone Society’s primary supplier of river and forestry resources. Dodo-powered Caravans travel between Erstburg and [["The Continent"#Newpoint|Newpoint]] constantly, prioritizing faster deliveries over heavier loads due to the constant threat of ambushes from Cinderpedes.

* Purpose: '''Freshwater Fishing''' and '''Forestry'''
* Established: '''2 Years Ago'''
* Population: '''24-31 People'''
* Story Character Inhabitants:
** [[Wynaquet]]

== Newpoint ==
{{ScriFa Location|title1=Newpoint|habitability=Moderate|climate_type=Boreal Fields|notable_resources=|current_factions=[[The Stone Society]]|historical_factions=|settlement_status=Dense Habitation|threat_level=None|resource_density=|anomaly=|image1=Newpoint OnMap.jpg}}

The newest and largest settlement of [[The Stone Society]].<br />Built along a jagged cliff facing [["The Spire"]], Newpoint is home to a young, industrious population dedicated to constructing, maintaining, and launching The Continent’s first Hot Air Balloon. With rows upon rows of stalls, wood houses, a makeshift clinic, storage facilities, and workshops, this bustling town is the closest the Stone Society would get to a worthy stone-age city.

Surrounding the platteau are rolling hills that give a tactical view of the village, with a single tent here hosting the local zealot and loner, [[Memetta]].

Its coastline is '''rocky''' and '''harsh''', but its high elevation grants unmatched visibility of the [["The Continent"#Fracturing Sea|Fracturing Sea]] and a direct path towards [["The Spire"]]. Although fishing is theoretically possible here, it's highly discouraged due to the treacherous steep staircase path going down to the shore.

*Purpose: '''Crafting''' and '''Hot Air Balloon Maintenance'''
* Established: '''5 Months Ago'''
* Population: '''38-43 People'''
* Story Character Inhabitants:
** [[Memetta]]

= Wilderness =

== Crossing Fields ==
{{ScriFa_Location|title1=Crossing Fields|habitability=Perfect|climate_type=Temperate Fields|notable_resources=Herbs, Berries, and small wildlife|current_factions=[[The Stone Society]]|settlement_status=Traderoute|threat_level=None|resource_density=Clustered}}
The continent’s largest biome — a '''broad temperate plain''' that links [["The Continent"#San Baciato|San Baciato]], [["The Continent"#Erstburg|Erstburg]], and [["The Continent"#Newpoint|Newpoint]], with it even encapsulating the northern edges of [["The Continent"#Dregwoods|Dregwoods]].

<br />The fields are open and grassy with few trees, abundant wild plants (notably wild '''hemp'''), and roving herds of dodo birds.  Caravans and wandering parties use the Crossing Fields as the main thoroughfare, and it is the first place most newcomers learn how unwieldy but free [["The Continent"]]'s wildlife can be.

*Purpose: '''Transportation'''
* Creatures: '''Dodo Birds'''

== Dregwoods ==
{{ScriFa_Location|title1=Dregwoods|image1=Dregwoods OnMap.jpg|habitability=Low|climate_type=Dark Pine Forest|notable_resources=Foraging, Hunting, Logging|anomaly=Mythillium|current_factions=Unclaimed|historical_factions=???|settlement_status=Isolated Individuals|threat_level=Medium-High|resource_density=Dense}}
A dense, '''temperate-to-boreal forest''' known for its eerie quiet. Common wildlife includes <u>squirrels</u>, <u>owls</u>, the occasional <u>bear</u>— and unusually, '''dodo birds'''.<br />Deeper within, explorers report finding animal corpses with strange, ''metal-like'' growths spreading through bone and flesh. Local rumors tie these “infections” to ancient, foreign technology— the sort of thing [[The Stone Society]] catalogues but rarely understands. However, [[Professor Besado]] seems quite adamant in saying it is "nothing to fear". Just don't venture too deep into these woods. At least, not alone.

If you do come <u>across these metallic growths</u>, it is imperative to '''avoid direct physical contact'''. Once collected, it is urgent that you deliver this to the man who lives near the woods' center. Look for [[Winchester]]. However, for any general injuries unrelated to Mythillium, [[Cassius Reed]] the local witchdoctor/shaman may provide adequate assistance.

*Purpose: '''Foraging''', '''Hunting''', and '''Logging'''
* Creatures: '''Dodo Birds''', '''Psyche Owls''', '''Bears''', '''Flying Squirrels''', '''Stags''', '''Mythillite Beasts'''
* Noteable Individuals:
** [[Winchester]]
** [[Cassius Reed]]

== The “Western Savannah” ==
{{ScriFa_Location|title1=The "Western Savannah"|habitability=Hazardeous|climate_type=Hilly Savannah|notable_resources=Coconuts|current_factions=Unclaimed|settlement_status=Abandoned|threat_level=High|resource_density=Sparse}}
A wide, barely-explored '''warm grassland''' northwest of [["The Continent"#Erstburg|Erstburg]].

<br />Expeditions into its bush frequently retreat due to attacks by [[Cinderpeddes|Cinderpedes]]— bear-sized blazing centipedes that burn and scatter like living embers. Smoke on the horizon is the only warning before a herd stampedes across the plains. Their strange, coordinated movements have inspired whispers that something— or someone— resonates with them.

Due to the lack of noteable resources there, interest to reclaim these lands has dwindled. However, recently, things seem to be changing...

== The Sweltertops ==
{{ScriFa_Location|title1=Sweltertops|image1=TheSweltertops OnMap.jpg|habitability=Low|climate_type=Mountainous Desert|notable_resources=- Trading <small>([[Wynaquette]])</small><br>
- Mining <small>(Undiscovered)</small>|anomaly=Extreme Heat|current_factions=Unclaimed|historical_factions=???|settlement_status=Isolated Individuals|threat_level=High|resource_density=Sparse}}
A deadly range of '''deserted-mountains'''— not mere dunes, but towering peaks where sweltering heat replaces snow. Sand shifts with alarming ease, causing '''sandvalanches''' to be a common occurance within these barely-mapped territories. Nearly nothing is known to survive here... except for [[Cinderpeddes|Cinderpedes]], of course.

There was no reason to clear travel into The Sweltertops. That was, until mineral fish were discovered downstream coming from these mountains. A cave with rare resources can be found buried somewhere in the sand. But until extensive expeditions and efforts to find it are made, this location will remain a mystery.

<small>''It is also noted, adventurers in these parts claim to feel as if they were being watched.''</small>

*Purpose: '''Mining''' <small>(undiscovered)</small>
* Creatures: '''Cinderpedes'''

= Misc Locations =

== “The Spire” ==
[[File:SpireCloseup.png|thumb|206x206px]]
An island in the middle of the Fracturing Sea, home to the massive structure known only to [[The Stone Society]] as "The Spire".

From the mainland, it appears distant and inscrutable; some swear they can make out rectangular silhouettes— ruined buildings or geological oddities. Nothing is certain until one arrives.  Any who travel close by sea, only return through echoes.

== Fracturing Sea ==
An ominous, deep, and outstretching body of seemingly-normal but eerily-calm water. Some locals avoid touching it; few claim its waters ''vibrate'' in an unsettling way. With no moon to guide tides, the sea often lies unnervingly still— moved mainly by wind. Yet, many believe there is something in its depths.  Maritime travel is perilous; those who attempt to cross the Fracturing Sea risk being consumed by phenomena that some survivors call '''Finality'''.

* Creatures: '''Fissuring Megalodon'''
