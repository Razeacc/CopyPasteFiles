###		Overarching Timeline
------------------------------------
The story of [[Scripted Fates]] can be split into '''three major eras''':<blockquote>[[Overarching Timeline#The Archaic Aeons|'''The Archaic Aeons''']]<br />The period when [[Archkin]] and [[Archkin Civilization]] ruled over mortals. They frequently lived among them and aided society in thriving under their developmental care.</blockquote><blockquote>[[Overarching Timeline#Revolutions and Exodi|'''Revolutions and Exodi''']]<br />Mortals revolt amidst dwindling attention from Archkin, leading to a mass chaos. The only Archkin civilization left standing after the [["The Revolutionaries"]] merciless [[Archkin]] hunts is [[Morgale de Metso]]'s [[The Avalonian Exodus|Avalonian Exodus]].</blockquote><blockquote>[[Overarching Timeline#Era of The Spirepocalypse|'''Era of The Spirepocalypse''']]<br />A mass catastrophe happens, shaking the world to its core. With [["The Spire"]] making its haunting appearance, [[Archkin]] and mortal alike seemingly lose all their memories, sending all who bare witness back to the stone age.</blockquote>
----

Each era has an '''entry''' explaining what happened, alongside a list of '''detailed historic events''' that took place during that time.<blockquote>If you are here for [[Firmata Frontier]], it is '''not required''' to catch yourself up to speed with the entire history of [[Scripted Fates]].<br />Instead, you may <u>skip straight ahead</u> to [[Overarching Timeline#Era of The Spirepocalypse|Era of The Spirepocalypse]] for the "''Full Firmata Memory Wipe''" experience. </blockquote>

[[File:Timeline 1.jpg|center|441x441px]]
=== Archaic Aeons Entry ===
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
Eons ago, two groups vied for dominion over the infinite fields: [[Mortals]] and [[Archkin]].

Their drive for innovation— unending, insatiable even. Even the divine couldn't endlessly sate the hunger of such unquenchable inventions. Although their immortal creators painstakingly sheltered their creations since time's first tick. That all changed when [["The Kissed One"]] challenged their court with her own— as its '''first puppet'''.

Debating and squabbling tirelessly, she'd insist on more attention and better treatment for mortals. [[The Archgodic Court]] protested, declaring they were stretched thin enough as is. The concept of [[Archgods]] having limitations— it was difficult enough for anyone to grasp, let alone someone with as much insatiable innovation as her.

Eventually, she cast all warning aside. When the girl drew upon two [[Beyonds]]— there stood an archgod, slain by mortal hands. From then on, the [[archkin]]<nowiki/> knew their thrones were not as impervious as they had prayed.

Henceforth, [[The Archgod Revolution|'''The Archgod Revolution''']] was set ablaze, a conflict reignited by the beckoning bells of heaven and hell.
</div>

=== Revolutions and Exodi Entry ===
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
As the uprising torched the infinite fields, [[archkin]] societies responded in their own manners.

The Archgod leaders of [[Marlika]] and [[Yan'amu]] vanished without a trace; their nations were consumed by the regretful cinders of freedom. The floating city carnivals that clouded doubt and woe with joy and whimsy: repurposed and forced to support the archkin survival efforts. Other nations either followed suit or rejoiced with [["The Revolutionaries"]].

The fates of captured [[archkin]] were scripted by one of three decrees: Sanctified and traitored, profaned then harvested, or recalculated— annulled. However, one archkin civilization always somehow escaped the revolutionaries. They were constantly tiptoeing on a hair's thread from ruin, acting as a true eyesore for their hunters: [[The Avalonian Exodus|'''The Avalonian Exodus''']].

As a nomadic nation, they travelled towards The 4th's Wall, ever pursued by their hunters. But when all hope seemed forlorn, as the frontlines closed in: '''Finality'''.
</div>

=== Rouse of Silence ===
<blockquote>''Year '''0''' PSP <small>(Post-[[The Spirepocalypse|Spirepocalypse]])</small>''
<div class="mw-collapsible">
Across the field, a blinding light burst forth beyond the frontier.<br />That was the final moment countless folk recall.
As time passed, one by one, the survivors regained their sense. However, aside from losing their common luxuries of modern life, so too were their memories stripped away. Mortal, [[Archkin]], no one was spared. [["The Continent"]]'s civilization has been sentenced to '''the Stone Age'''.

Without memories to guide them, or words to sooth their confusions, all they had to look up to was a certain stain on the horizon. There, that beam of light continued pouring infinite glimmer up into the deep sky. As if mocking them down below, [["The Spire"]] stood as a testament for their new era of struggle; the last and most obvious remnant of the old world, forever seeming just out of reach.

''Year '''16''' PSP <small>(Post-[[The Spirepocalypse|Spirepocalypse]])</small>''

Society has started picking itself back up— at least by a little. Their efforts and hardships formed [[San Baciato|the first settlement]], built atop walls of mud and thatched roofs. By day, people watch the dim sky brighten, going about their routines, from collecting materials and gathering food, to basking in nature or conversing with colleagues. Then, as night rolls around, the sky darkens. Glitters of light twinkle down from the sky at random patterns each night. Villagers gaze into the deep unknown, ignorantly blissful of their moonless twilights.
</div>
</blockquote>

=== Arrival of The [["Otherworlders"]] ===
<blockquote>''Year '''17''' PSP <small>(Post-[[The Spirepocalypse|Spirepocalypse]])</small>''
<div class="mw-collapsible">
One night, while gazing between the deep blue and The Spire, a comet would fly out from the infinite pillar of light.
Coated in a similar glimmer of that mysterious stain, these shooting stars occasionally shot across The Continent.<br />Eventually, one landed near a town.

<small>No crash was heard, no ripple was felt.<br />Where this comet landed: A body.<br />Moving? Breathing? Talking!<br />No pain was felt.</small> 

Locals would gawk in awe, question, and even interrogate. Those from The Spire had knowledge— vast, impossible knowledge. From lands unheard of, to things forever unseen. Each had their own stories too, further adding to the locals' confusion. The visitors too shared in the confusion, baffled by the lack of celestial bodies in The Continent's sky. ''Here be no sun nor moon. Merely, the sky itself— lighting or dimming to its own heightened tune.''

Eventually, puzzle pieces clicked together in everyone's heads, and these new arrivals were given a title: [["Otherworlder"]].
</div>
</blockquote>
=== Journey to [["The Spire"]] ===
<blockquote>Year '''20''' PSP <small>(Post-[[The Spirepocalypse|Spirepocalypse]])</small>
<div class="mw-collapsible">
Soon, [[The Stone Society]] prepares to take flight.
Two new towns were established, with one focused solely on constructing the survivors' most ambitious project yet: '''The Hot Air Balloon'''.
Using knowledge from their home worlds, and the locals' experience on The Continent, they plan to venture towards that infinite beacon of light, seeking solutions to questions they've long left unsolved.

The epicenter of The Spirepocalypse, presumed cause of everyone's stripped memories, and source of The [["Otherworlders"]]; there must lay an unending treasure trove of answers.
</div>
</blockquote>
