# Idea
## Overview
Acting as multiversal read/write Singletons across all of fiction, Epicenters are the pinnacle backbones of ideas and phenomenon across The Worlds Aplenty.
Although most Epicenters are unbound and holder-less, the few that do have holders grant them unparalleled access and privilege, at the cost of immense strain.

Epicenterhood subjecrs yourself to "Reading" every single occurence of the idea you're an Epicenter of.
- The Epicenter of Sentience would be aware of every single life across all of fiction, and how that life is feeling (due to how sentience is defined in this world as the capability to process memories... also yes, this is how Memetta knows where everyone is).
If one thrives through the sensory overload, and with enough skill, they can even "Write" into all occurances of that idea.
- The Epicenter of Spatial could edit any part of space wherever it is (this is how Vektat creates Firmatas while guarding the spatial borders himself... or, at least that's how he should be doing it).
Epicenters are the gravity awells of ideas and phenomena, allowing them to flow from the abstract to reality.

Furthermore, you may often see Epicenters referred to as "Concepts". **Concepts are no different from Epicenters**, it's merely a more poetic secondary terminology to contrast "Epicenter"'s scientific / literal definition.

### Metaphor
This alteration is similar to "Concept" and "Epicenter", where it replaces the actual adjective of the title.
However, unlike the two meaning the same thing (just with the former being the more poetic name and later being its scientific name), being a Metaphor entitles oneself to absolutely zero benefits or downsides— they merely embody the idea they "Metaphorize" to the absolute best of their abilities. They cannot perceive all instances of that idea, similar to Epicenters and Concepts.
Instead, it seems something greater awaits the Metaphors down the line— as a Metaphor it's made without purpose.

## Boundless / Unbound
Merely the term used for Epicenters that have no holder.
You will never see someone with a Boundless Epicenter/Concept... under normal circumstances.

(On an additional side note: Carriers of Epicenters are called "Holders" by default, but the term can change to match the Epicenter better.
Such as the Concept of Memories' holder being a "Recollector". Or the Epicenter of Simulation being a "Singleton".)

Chatty's Memory Notes:
Epicenterhood refers to the process through which concepts, emotions, identities, or symbolic ideas accumulate enough psychological and narrative density to manifest as semi-stable entities.
Epicenters and Concepts are the same thing. Epicenter is merely the Formal definition, while Concepts are the Metaphorical/Poetic term.

An Epicenter is not merely powerful.
It is the emotional "center of gravity" for a concept. When something embodies an Epicenter, they become the central "flow" that allows that idea to manifest. 
Think of it as a "Read/Write" process. An Epicenter can "Read" EVERY single instance of the idea they're an Epicenter for. And, with enough skill, they can even "Write" to/through those instances as well.

Examples include:
- Epicenter of Fiction (Ulhysse)
> Witnesses and Feels every single fictional creature, object, idea, material, surface, person, character, god, cell, germ, food, plant, etc. across all of fiction, from the very beginning to very end of time, all at once, all the time.
- Epicenter of Sentience (Memetta)
> Sees "sentience" and is fully aware of where everyone is and what they're feeling at the time. Isn't aware WHY they're feeling those ways, though. She can see every single result, but is completely oblivious to the forumlas.
- Concept of Self-Destruction (Besado, temporarily in the ending of Firmata Frontier)
- Concept of Sacrifice (Memetta following Firmata Frontier's ending)
- Epicenter of Concepts (The 4th Beyond)
> Allows the creation of new Epicenters via "witnessing" them.

Epicenters are often born through:
- collective belief
- emotional concentration
- repeated historical reinforcement
- symbolic inheritance
- trauma accumulation
Once "witnessed" by the Epicenter of Concepts (writers/viewers/readers/players), that acts as a trigger for a new Epicenter to be brought into existence.

Concepthood is often considered dangerous, as an entity ceases functioning as a normal individual and instead becomes partially indistinguishable from the idea they embody.

This process can blur:
- individuality
- mortality
- emotional boundaries
- reality consistency

Some entities fear Concepthood more than death itself.
