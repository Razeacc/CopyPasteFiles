# Overview
Following The Spirepocalypse, when Nul Detta begun breaking down Khueen further in order to test his hypothesis that "If Khueen is NOT the Epicenter of Psyche, but rather, the Heretical Epicenter of Psyche, that means I (Nul Detta) can become a true Epicenter, rather than a Metaphore, of Psyche by destroying or absorbing Khueen". That is the main dilemma of The Spirepocalypse.
By organizing The Living Piece of Morgale, The Simuling of Kaitzar, and many other potential unnamed actors, Nul Detta secretly orchestrated The Spirepocalypse even under the noses of their own vicars and children, all for the sake of one massive distraction from his main goal of becoming the Epicenter of Psyche.
The result of his experiment was the shattering of Psyche Planes altogether. This lowered the floor to manifest and edit a Psyche Plane, alongside raising its skill ceiling by making it less restrictive.
While Psyche Duels are still possible, its limits and restructions have been shattered in this new replacement for it, something that now stains The Era of The Spirepocalypse:
Overwrite.
It is essentially a Psyche User's own Fracturing Zone or NulGraven Site: a physical space that's an extension of one's psyche into the physical realm. For amateurs, it merely paints or "overwrites" the world. But for more experienced users, it can potentially even add or superimpose itself onto the terrain to create new temporary structures. Overwrites are also generally weaker than Psyche Planes/Duels, as the added real-world materialization expends some focus that would've been used in a more potent Psyche Plane.
(Also, all information regarding why Overwrites became a thing following The Spirepocalypse is currently a mystery. As far as everyone's concerned, people just started developing Psyche Planes more rapidly, and being able to "somehow" leak them out of their bodies ever since The Spirepocalypse.)

## Differences between Psyche Duels and Overwrites
While Psyche Duels happened within the plane itself and caused no high-stakes consequences, Overwrites pulls the Psyche Plane into reality. Time continues to flow, physical damage is retained, and the Ovewrite itself is highly unstable comprared to planes. The longer an Overwrite is maintained, the more instrusive thoughts and deep secrets leak out into the surface, potentially hindering even the user of the Overwrite. This differs greatly from Psyche Planes and Duels, which are more stable in comparison.
However, just like Psyche Planes, not all Overwrites are combat-focused, as some Overwrites can instead be used to help find clues, reveal footprints, display "interactables" (commonly used by videogame character-controlled skribbles), create temporary resting points, etc.

## How to inscribe an Overwrite
First, a user needs to be **aware of Overwrites** in the first place, awareness is key, similar to how awareness of spell incantations shortcuts is important to actually use spell shortcuts. Overwrite is, in of itself, a type of "global spell", after all.
Second, depending on which "Frontier Stat" you prioritize (or which one you personally prioritize), **make a certain gesture** (custom gestures being possible if you can't make any of these motions, it just needs to be a "pose that reflects yourself the most").
*   **PHYSICALITY**: Press your fists together or "reload" your fists (punching them outwards)
*   **ALACRITY**: Close or squint your eyes
*   **MENTALITY**: Press a finger to your temple or press a fist to your chin in a "thinking" motion
*   **SPIRITUALITY**: Grasp your heart, or pump your chest
Third, **shout "Overwrite"**, which acts similarly to a spell. It commands the archose within their soul to inscribe the psyche pane outside of their body and into the world, overwriting the world around them.

Those with a soul capable of witnessing the incantation (hear, feel, see, sense, etc.) will then feel the user's soul stretch out from their eyes and into the world around them, like massive flowing flows of ink decorating a canvas. And just like that, a psyche plane has now overwritten the physical world.
