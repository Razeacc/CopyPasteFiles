== [[Firmata Frontier]] (November 2025) ==
[[File:FirmataFrontier Poster.png| link=Firmata Frontier |thumb|250x250px|border]]

Firmata Frontier is a [https://discord.com Discord] Roleplaying server where members could directly '''participate''' and '''interact''' with the world of [[Scripted Fates]]. Not only that, it also finally pieces together all the bits of lore that have been scattered around the previous projects thus far, alongside all the content found on the official [https://www.youtube.com/c/@razeacc YouTube channel]. In a sense, Firmata Frontier acts as the catalyst for all Scripted Fates content beginning to line up.

The story takes place in a completely new location and quite some time after the events of [[Farewell Avalon]]. [[Roleplayer Characters]] find themselves on the frontlines of [[The Avalonian Exodus]]'s [[Outpost 3]], the largest labyrinth of the nomadic civilization. With the land still suffering from the catastrophic memory-wiping affects of [[The Spirepocalypse]], players must work together to rebuild modern life while uncovering the mysteries behind such a calamity.

== [[Hinokami Caretaker]] (October 2023) ==
[[File:CaretakerCover small.png|thumb|279x279px]]

Acting as the first ''written'' entry in the [[Scripted Fates]] continuity, Hinokami Caretaker is a short novel spanning 2 chapters. The short story functions as the 3rd entry to the [[Scripted Fates]] series.

It follows the story of [[Ouya]] after getting an order from [[Khueen]] to watch over and raise one of her 'creations': a human boy name [[Grigori]]. Hinokami Caretaker shows the struggle of a god-like being attempting to relate and care for a mortal as if it were her own child. This entry showcases the first appearance of [[Cradles]]. 




== [[Farewell Avalon]] (May 2023) ==
[[File:FarewellAvalon 3-4.jpg|thumb|243x243px]]

Being the first fully-interactive and explorable entry in the series, Farewell Avalon is a '''top-down bullet hell adventure game''' following the story of [[Archie]], a newly-born [[Archkin]]. As the 2nd entry to the series, following [[Beyond Eternity]], it takes place in an entirely new location with an entirely separate cast of characters and factions. Namely, introducing [[The Avalonian Exodus]] and one of its labyrinths: [[Outpost 7]].

However, [[Archie]] won't be travelling alone, as they were accompanied by either [[The Skribble]] or [[Protos]], both agents from [[The Court of The 4th]] acting as a development of the concept for The Player ([[You]])'s influence initially being introduced in [[Beyond Eternity]].


== [[Beyond Eternity]] (February 2020) ==
[[File:Beyond Eternity poster final v2.jpg|border|367x367px|right]]The first official entry to the [[Scripted Fates]] series, Beyond Eternity is a Visual Novel following the story of three [[Archgods]] following their banishment to [[The Null Crossing]]: [[Khueen]], [[Ouya]], and [[Crimmy]]. At first, you're lead to believe that you'd be playing as someone, but not long after beginning the game, it's revealed [[You]] play as '''yourself'''. Meanwhile, the avatar you control is the body of a completely separate character: [[Kalayan]]. The goal of the game is to somehow separate your control from Kalayan to reunite him with Khueen.

Firmata Frontier is a '''Discord Roleplaying''' '''server''' where members could directly participate and interact with the world of [[Scripted Fates]]. It acts as the 4th entry to the series, and its 3rd Discord server, with the previous two acting more like 'art hubs'.



== Introduction ==
Following the appearance of [["The Spire"]], everyone's memories have been '''wiped clean''', destroying any concept of modernity across the lands, marking the era of [[The Spirepocalypse]].

Some time after, comet-like lights started falling from [["The Spire"]]. These were [["Otherworlders"]], people from different universes. Unlike the [["Local"|"Locals"]] affected by [[The Spirepocalypse]], [["Otherworlders"|Otherworlders]] were seemingly unaffected, retaining most of their memories, having only lost powerful abilities and equipment.

Together, Roleplayers could join the story either as an [["Otherworlder"]] or [["Local"]]. They must work together to bring back the modern age, uncover the mysteries that have ruined these lands, and maybe even find a way back home.

== Gameplay ==
After passing the Verified RPer assessment, Roleplayers may use the [https://docs.google.com/document/d/1KiGHpZTePSU9A3_6OCjrDm2qfDmElD3aobHcTB7d-eY/edit?usp=drive_link Firmata Character Template] to '''create a character'''. This character would join the roster of [[Player Characters]].
----

=== <big>[[Firmata's Rolling System]] '''{{Glimmer|full}}'''</big> ===
[[Firmata Frontier]] uses its own [[Firmata's Rolling System|Stat/Rolling System]] to determine the outcome of certain actions. Although, this system '''isn't mandatory''' by default and may be used either if <u>both Roleplayers agree</u>, or if <u>insisted by a Narrator</u>.

More information can be found in the system's [[Firmata's Rolling System|dedicated page]].
----

===  <big>Roleplay Styles</big> ===
Once a character has been '''submitted''' and '''approved''', the Roleplayer may now begin Roleplaying. There are 4 options available from the get-go.

==== '''<big>Casual Roleplay</big>''' ====
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
<blockquote>This happens in <u>certain open-forum threads</u> where Roleplayers can hop in and out whenever they want. <u>No major developments</u> are intended here, opening up the opportunity for simple, casual, and low-stress character bond development.<br />This is also typically where '''Resource Acquisition''' can be done where Roleplayers '''gamble/roll''' in certain locations to either <u>fish</u>, <u>forage</u>, or <u>collect</u> certain materials.  </blockquote>
</div>

==== '''<big>Episodic Roleplay</big>''' ====
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
<blockquote>This is done on forums where Roleplayers '''open new location-based threads'''. From there, Roleplayers take turn replying to each other on a regular basis with the '''goal'''. A <u>Narrator may be present</u> here as well to monitor and act as a third party for any world occurrences.<br />This is best saved for '''Expeditions''' to chart out new territories, attempts at '''reinventing technology''', or fulfilling '''Quests'''.</blockquote>
</div>

==== '''<big>Personal Roleplay</big>''' ====
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
<blockquote>Entirely optional, Roleplayers can do a wide variety of fun extra activities from writing <u>diaries/journals</u>, setting up <u>homes</u>, or even <u>flashbacks</u> and non-canon "what if" threads.</blockquote>
</div>

==== '''<big>Event Roleplay</big>''' ====
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
<blockquote>Occasionally, the Event forum would open up to a wide variety of different Episodic and Casual Roleplay opportunities. Events occasionally have Event Shops as well, where Roleplayers can earn "Scripting Ink" during the event to spend in these shops.

When purchasing using "Scripting Ink", [[You]] manipulate fate for the character to somehow <u>receive the item</u> you bought for them.

Events are typically '''major plot-progressing opportunities''' where something massive would happen that may unlock things from <u>new explorable areas</u> to <u>new mechanics</u> to play around with.</blockquote>
</div>

== Factions ==
The only known group of survivors currently are known as [[The Stone Society|'''The Stone Society''']].

Led by [[Professor Besado]], since being the first newborn following [[The Spirepocalypse]], her naturally-innovative mind has skyrocketed the world's tech from being completely Neolithic to having some semblance to Medieval technologies in merely 19 years.

== Setting ==
Taking place in a '''wild''' and '''untamed wilderness''' currently known as simply [["The Continent"]], it teams with all sorts of prehistoric wildlife. Notably, many extinct creatures from [[You|Your]] world seem to thrive in these lands— from dodo birds and wooly mammoths, to a mysterious but notorious leviathan prowling the [[Fracturing Sea|seas]].

The world itself is quite odd, especially to an [["Otherworlder"]]. However, to a [["Local"]], they may not even bat an eye to the fact that the '''skies feature no sun nor moon whatsoever'''. In fact, even the '''stars often change positions''' every night, with no constellation pattern in sight. Despite how bizarre the sky is, there is '''still a day/night cycle''', with the sky merely <u>brightening or dimming</u> to mimic typical mornings and evenings.

There's a map of what's <u>currently explored</u>, letting Roleplayers '''discover''' and '''mark''' territories on their own.

Farewell Avalon is a '''2D''' '''Top-Down Adventure Bullet Hell''' where you control [[The Skribble]] and [[Protos]] as they lead the main protagonist, [[Archie]], along on their journey to reclaim their lost memories and find where the rest of their kin have vanished off to.

It's the 2nd project of the [[Scripted Fates]] series, following [[Beyond Eternity]], and the first entry involving the Unity game engine.

== Introduction ==
After waking up from being reborn as an [[Archkin]], [[Archie]] is faced by either [[The Skribble]] or [[Protos]], both being [[Skribbles]]. They lead the newly-born archkin across [[Outpost 7]] as they reclaim [[Memory Pieces]] from their past as a human. During which, they face the outpost's deeply-infested [[Fracturing Zone]] and the [[cradles]] within, alongside the abandoned [[Cariburn]] that still roam its halls.

== Gameplay ==
In the first version of Farewell Avalon, you can control [[Archie]]'s movement while using the mouse to aim their melee attacks. After reaching low-health, [[The Skribble]] steps in to let Archie rest temporarily: The Skribble Switch. During that rest, you play as the skribble companion as you defend Archie while they regain health. Your melee attacks also swap with a ranged attack instead.

In the second version, Project: FRACTURING, defeating enemies charges up "Faith" [[Protos]] has with Archie's capabilities to defend themselves. Instead of The Skribble Switch occurring on low health, it can be triggered at any time, but it slowly drains "Faith". Rather than defending Archie as well, you swap places with them and control the fast-moving skribble for the time being. This creates a balancing dynamic of melee primary attacks to charge "ammunition" to swap into your secondary ranged attacks.

####		Firmata's Rolling System
........................................

The '''Firmata Rolling System''' is the universal resolution mechanic used across the '''[[Firmata Frontier]]''' roleplay server. It determines the '''difficulty''' of actions, the '''success''' of attempts, and the '''personal power level''' of both Story Character and [[Player Characters]].

The system '''blends''' <u>DnD-Styled</u> and  <u>Narrative Roleplaying</u> systems, finding a middle-ground between the ''complexity'' of the prior and ''freedom'' of the later. However, Firmata's Rolling System is '''narrative-first''', with rolls only functioning as a '''guide''' for <u>how to write a response</u>. There is '''no hard-coded health''' tracking. [[You]] decide how much your character can handle.

Featuring '''modularity''' allowing for both <u>character growth</u> and <u>equipment</u> opportunities, it is '''balanced''' for both world-native characters and multiverse arrivals entering [["The Continent"]].


== Rolling Mechanic ==
When performing rolls during Roleplay, send a message formatted like this without anything else:


The bot reads messages that start with a roll, and labels it with what you "rolled for"
 (roll) + (roll label)
 d20+7 Cinderpede Attack PHYS


'''You''' <u>write a response</u> for your '''attack''', then '''both you''' and the '''defender''' <u>roll</u>. Then the '''defender''' <u>writes a response</u> for their '''defense.'''

After, the '''Defender''' <u>writes a response</u> for their next '''attack'''. You '''both roll''', then '''you''' <u>write a response</u> for your '''defense'''.

Since this system is '''narrative first''', the roll results only determine <u>how a response will be written</u>.<br />Low rolls that may ''seem out of character'' can be equated to external factors like '''bad luck''', with the inverse being true for good rolls.

=== Combat Rolling ===
There are 2 typical scenarios you can find yourself in during combat: '''Melee''' or '''Ranged'''.

==== Melee Rolling ====
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
The '''Attacker''' rolls 💪[[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|Physicality]] to <u>launch their attack</u>.<br />The '''Defender''' rolls '''💪[[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|Physicality]]''' to <u>block</u> or <u>dodge</u> the attack.<br />'''Attacker''' →  '''Higher roll''' = The attack '''hits''', and the <u>difference</u> determines <u>damage severity</u>.<br />'''Defender''' →  '''Higher roll''' = The attack is either '''blocked''' or '''dodged''', with the <u>difference</u> determining <u>block</u> or <u>dodge effectiveness</u>.
</div>

==== Ranged Rolling ====
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
The '''Attacker''' rolls ⚡[[Firmata's Rolling System#⚡ Alacrity (ALC)|Alacrity]] to <u>aim their projectile</u>.<br />The D'''efender''' can either roll '''💪[[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|Physicality]]''' or ⚡[[Firmata's Rolling System#⚡ Alacrity (ALC)|Alacrity]] to <u>dodge</u> or <u>react</u> to the projectile.<br />'''Defender''' →  '''Higher roll''' = The projectile misses entirely and it's now '''the defender's turn''' to respond.<br />'''Attacker''' →  '''Higher roll''' = Both parties <u>roll again</u>.<br />This time, '''the Attacker''' rolls for 💪[[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|Physicality]] to <u>determine the projectile's strength</u> <small>(for '''gun users''', <u>only roll the gun's physicality</u>, character physicality '''does not''' affect gun strength)</small>.<br />Meanwhile, '''the defender''' rolls '''💪[[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|Physicality]]''' to <u>determine damage severity</u>.
</div>

== Core Stats ==
There are '''four fundamental stats''', each representing a trait in a character's capability.<br />💪[[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|Physicality]] | ⚡[[Firmata's Rolling System#⚡ Alacrity (ALC)|Alacrity]] | 💡[[Firmata's Rolling System#💡 Mentality (MENT)|Mentality]] | 🌙[[Firmata's Rolling System#🌙 Spirituality (SPIR)|Spirituality]]

----

<blockquote>''Raw bodily capability. strength, force, endurance, '''agility''', athleticism, pain resistance, and overall physical impact. <br />This determines what '''your body is capable of'''.''</blockquote>
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
* <small>Melee Aggression, Defense, and '''Speed''' <sup>(You need strong legs to run fast, after all)</sup></small>
* <small>Body-based feats</small>
* <small>Lifting, pushing, resisting</small>
* <small>Dodging physically</small>
* <small>Endurance tests</small>
</div>



=== ⚡ Alacrity (ALC) ===
<blockquote>''Reaction time, finesse, coordination, balance, accuracy, and movement precision.<br />This determines what you can '''make your body do'''.''</blockquote>
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
* <small>Aimed attacks</small>
* <small>Ranged accuracy</small>
* <small>Sleight of hand</small>
* <small>Precision dodging</small>
* <small>Quick reflex maneuvers</small>
* <small>Technique-based combat styles</small>
</div>



=== 💡 Mentality (MENT) ===
<blockquote>''Intellect, reasoning, focus, creativity, planning, memory, analysis, and rational decision making.<br />This determines '''what your brain can do'''.''</blockquote>
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
* <small>Strategy</small>
* <small>Investigation</small>
* <small>Solving problems</small>
* <small>Item/Equipment creation</small>
* <small>Complex techniques that require mental upkeep</small>
</div>



=== 🌙 Spirituality (SPIR) ===
<blockquote>''Soul intuition, emotions, wisdom, inner power, spiritual discipline, supernatural attunement, and magical/ritualistic practices.<br />This determines '''what your soul can do'''.''</blockquote>
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
* <small>Magic/[[Archkin]] abilities</small>
* <small>Manipulating [[The 4 Aspects of Reality]]</small>
* <small>Emotional resilience</small>
* <small>[[PSYCHE (Soul)|Soul]] pressure</small>
* <small>[[Faith]]-based or Spirit-attuned effects</small>
* <small>[[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Phantasy|Psychic]]/Empathic interactions</small>
* <small>Aura Projection</small>
* <small>Morality</small>
</div>


== {{Glimmer|full}} Resonance ==
'''Resonance''' determines how close a character has ''resonated with their fate'', how much they've '''achieved their''' '''full potential'''.<br />Typically, <u>younger</u> or <u>less experienced</u> characters get '''lower resonance''', indicating they still have '''lots of potential'''.<br />Meanwhile, <u>veteran</u> or <u>older</u> characters have '''higher resonance''', showing they've '''already peaked''' in potential.  

Resonance determines 2 key factors:  

# How many '''initial [[Firmata's Rolling System#Core Stats|stat points]]''' can be allocated <u>upon character creation</u>.
# How '''often [[Firmata's Rolling System#Resonance Attunement (Leveling Up)|new stat points]] may be granted''' by <u>Narrators</u> upon <u>character growth</u> moments.

=== Resonance Attunement (Leveling Up) ===
Characters can '''increase Resonance''' and gain more '''[[Firmata's Rolling System#Core Stats|stat points]]''' only through '''major narrative development''' — revelations, trauma, evolution, transformation, self-understanding, etc. These new points are given by [[Narrators]].

There are 2 kinds of new stat points:

# '''Growth Point''' → Reward for character development
# '''Resonance Realignment →''' When achieving a higher resonance tier, gain a bonus point depending on the achieved tier.

===== Resonance Realignment Example =====
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
<blockquote><small>Character goes from '''3{{Glimmer|full}} → 4{{Glimmer|full}} Resonance''':</small>

* <small>Starting Points for 3{{Glimmer|full}} = 3 points</small>
* <small>Starting Points for 4{{Glimmer|full}} = 5 points</small>
* '''<small>Difference = +2 points</small>'''
* <small>Plus '''+1 Growth Point'''</small>   
'''<small>Total: +3 points</small>'''

<small>But for '''2{{Glimmer|full}} → 3{{Glimmer|full}}''' '''Resonance''':</small>

* <small>Starting(2{{Glimmer|full}}) = 2 points</small>
* <small>Starting(3{{Glimmer|full}})= 3 points</small>
* <small>Difference = +1</small>
* <small>Plus Growth = +1</small>  
'''<small>Total: +2 points</small>'''
</blockquote>
</div>

=== Resonance Regression (Leveling Down) ===
On '''RARE CIRCUMSTANCES''' Characters do have a chance to '''lose resonance''' during certain character development moments, or if their [[memory]] gets wiped <small>(such as what happened in [[The Spirepocalypse]])</small>. If this were to ever happen, the only points lost are those granted from '''Resonance Realignment'''. Points gifted for generic character growth are '''permanent'''. 

Once <u>resonance returns to normal</u>, the reclaimed point/s may be '''reallocated''' to a <u>different [[Firmata's Rolling System#Core Stats|stat]] .</u>

== Equipment ==

=== Melee Tools ===
These items typically grant boosts to the [[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|💪 Physicality]] stat when used for their '''intended purpose'''. As this is for roleplay, '''the intended purpose can vary and be extended''' if <u>justified well enough</u>. A sword, intended for slashing, can still give its stat boost when cutting through leaves or webs.

=== Ranged Tools ===
For '''Bows''', '''Slings''', or any other ranged tool that '''requires the character's strength''', <u>character [[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|💪 Physicality]] is added by the tool's [[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|💪 Physicality]]</u>. <br />But for '''guns''' or any other ranged tool that '''doesn't need character strength''', you <u>'''only roll''' with the '''tool's [[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|💪 Physicality]]'''</u> . <small>(To balance this, guns typically have higher stats than bows.)</small>

=== Armor ===
Armor typically blocks [[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|💪 Physicality]] rolls against your character. However, optionally, '''damage types''' and '''armor coverage''' can be used as well.


===== Damage Types (Optional) =====
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
When enabled by both parties or a Narrator, damage types determine how well '''Slashing''', '''Piercing''', '''Blunt''', '''Heat''', or '''Psyche''' damage is mitigated by armor or caused by weapons. As this is a complication of the system, it is left to the Roleplayers to determine whether to use this or not.
</div>

===== Armor Coverage (Optional) =====
<div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed">
When enabled by both parties or a Narrator, <u>damage resistance</u> is '''only counted if an attack is directed at a body part that the armor covers'''. Even if a <u>helmet</u> gives +3 [[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|💪 Physicality]], if the attack is <u>aimed for the leg</u>, you <u>only roll your character's [[Firmata's Rolling System#💪 Physicality (PHY)|💪 Physicality]]</u> without the helmet's modifier.
</div>

{{AspectIcon|4}} '''[[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Phantasy|Phantasy]]''' is easily the '''most complicated aspect''' as it almost single-handedly governs the entirety of '''non-existence''' and '''abstract''' nature, only gaining assistance from {{AspectIcon|3}} [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Control|CONTROL]] in order to maintain the '''frequencies''' of [[PSYCHE (Soul)]] phenomena and [[Memories#Memory Amplitude|'''amplitude''']] of [[Memories]]. == Overview ==


Memory is a resource <u>embodied by anything and everything</u> that can '''think''' and '''choose''' for oneself. It is what's contained and utilized by ⁠[[Sentience]] in the formation of ⁠[[PSYCHE (Soul)|PSYCHE Energy]] ('''Soul''').<blockquote><small>Memory is governed by the [[Fracturing]] half of {{Phantasy}} [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Phantasy|Phantasy]].</small></blockquote>

== Memory Contents ==
Aside from the textbook definition of "Memories", it contains '''more''' than merely 'moments of time'.

Memories are '''innate'''. They contain '''Experiences''', '''Abilities''', and '''Natural Instincts'''. In <u>rare cases</u>, they can be '''passed down to offspring''', usually in a '''locked''' state. Offspring would need to unlock inherited memories to access them.<blockquote>{{#SQuote:|text=Knowing how to <u>breath</u>, how to <u>beat</u> your own <b>heart</b>, when to <u>blink</u>, <b>how</b> to blink, when you <u>think</u>—<br />how a bird <u>flies</u>, why your life <u>flashes before your eyes</u>, and how Archkin <u>manipulate</u> and <u>twist</u> their <b>lies</b>.|speaker=[[An Agent]]|receiver=|attribution=|source=|id=WLYA-NL6T-1A2J|template=}}</blockquote>Memories are the '''Instructions''' telling your soul what to do and how to do things. Whether or not you can actually process said instructions, and how well you process them, depends on your [[sentience]].

== Extracting Memories ==
Normally, you may simply '''ask someone''' holding certain memories to <u>retell</u> what they have to say.<br />Or, you could be '''bold''' and '''dastardly''', doing something '''not many others dare to do''' in the modern age:<br /><u>Read a book</u>.

However, if you mean how to '''extract''' memories, the process is a lot more hideous that you may expect.
[[File:Memory Piece Maker.png|thumb]]

=== [[Memory Pieces]] ===
The method to acquire ⁠[[Memory Pieces]] is quite the '''delicate''' procedure, as you need to extract both a <u>sliver of ⁠[[Sentience]]</u> while '''actively thinking of the memory''' or experience you wish to extract. This process is proven to be even more difficult for '''subconscious''' memories, such knowing how to <u>breath</u> or <u>beat</u> your own heart.

A ''certain apparatus'' is required, and it must be lodged into one's eye, the gateway to one's ⁠[[PSYCHE (Soul)|Soul]], to begin the process. More information can be found in the dedicate page to [[Memory Pieces]].

Extracting a memory does '''not''' always remove it from the host—though certain procedures, mishandled, are known to leave fragments or gaps. Bits of a memory may <u>stay and regrow</u>, and some extra memories <u>may be extracted as well</u>. The process isn't perfect when handled by '''mortals''', with a lot of stakes riding on mere random chance.

== Memory Amplitude ==
If [[PSYCHE (Soul)]] relies on unique frequencies to determine its color, Memories utilize '''Amplitude''' instead. Amplitude determines '''how'''  [[Sentience]] should depict the memories, acting similar to Volume/Loudness in Sound Physics.<blockquote>Memory amplitude is influenced by <u>emotional intensity</u>, <u>clarity</u>, and the <u>age</u> of the memory. However, the system remains poorly understood currently with further analysis pending.</blockquote>

== Overview ==
Archkin are beings made of two parts: '''Soul''' and '''Faith''' tied together with '''Frequencies'''.

=== Soul Energy ===
The [[PSYCHE (Soul)|SOUL]] comes in two halves: [[Memory]] and [[Sentience]]. They use [[PSYCHE (Soul)|Soul Energy]] as the '''building blocks''' for their capabilities in the form of "''Archose''", a <u>dust-like substance</u> that makes up their shapeshifting form. This is a '''heightened extension''' from how [[memories]] act as the building blocks for [[PSYCHE (Soul)|soul]] energy. 

This means they '''gain more soul energy''' the <u>more [[memories]]</u> and <u>experiences</u> they obtain. 

But in contrast, how does an Archkin ''heighten their [[Sentience]]'' to match the increased [[memories]]? Archkin eventually becomes cognitively unbalanced when overloaded with <u>[[memories]]</u> while lacking the [[sentience]] stability to interpret them. There is a way to heighten [[sentience]].

=== Faith ===
[[Faith]] is a '''resource''' innate in <u>anything containing a soul</u>. It's just that Archkin are more capable of tapping into it. By <u>challenging their confidence</u> and '''succeeding''', they gain [[Faith]] in themselves. This '''heightens their [[sentience]]''', increasing their overall prowess and capabilities. By completing a god-like feat, this is also what elevates an Archkin's [[Archkin#Archose|archose]], ascending it '''even further''', and achieving [[Archgods|Archgod]]<nowiki/>hood.

Although, a word of caution is in store. As their [[sentience]] heightens, their emotionality deepens as well— making them more susceptible to overwhelming states such as '''[[Archkin#The Pandemonium State|Pandemonium]]'''.

=== Frequencies ===
All Archkin resonate at '''unique frequencies''', similar to how [[PSYCHE (Soul)|SOULs]] and their natural/resonant frequencies. If <u>humans</u> have '''''DNA''''', <u>Archkin</u> have '''''frequencies'''''. These frequencies form an invisible protective field around them, '''shielding them from physical harm'''. However, when that frequency is disturbed by external stimuli, it <u>weakens</u> their protection, but only ''slightly''.<br />Their <u>true weaknesses</u> resides in the {{Nul Sym}} [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Phantasy|'''Phantasmal''']] '''Frequency''' of '''2413Hz''' and {{4th Sym}} [[You|'''Your''']] '''3142Hz:''' the <u>separatist frequencies</u>.

When afflicted by any of those frequencies, it forcibly <u>tears apart their body's archose</u> and <u>weakens their frequency shell</u>. The feeling is akin to being shocked by electricity while burning alive.<br />During this state, that is the '''only time''' an archkin may suffer '''finality'''. '''Frequencies''' act as the '''bond''' uniting an Archkin's [[PSYCHE (Soul)|Soul]] and [[Faith]] together. But when faced with ''separation'', it becomes their '''greatest weakness'''.

== Abilities ==

=== Manipulation of [[The 4 Aspects of Reality]] {{AspectIcon|1}}{{AspectIcon|2}}{{AspectIcon|3}}{{AspectIcon|4}} ===
By using their body's [[Archkin#Archose|archose]] physiology, they can '''manipulate reality''' through various manners. <u>Heating up ground</u>, <u>splitting air</u>, <u>capturing sentience</u>, and even <u>rewriting memories</u>. They are essentially made of "magic", some may describe it. There is virtually nothing they can't do <small>(a misconception that was the leading-cause for [[The Revolutions and Exodi Era|The Archgod Revolution]]).</small>

=== Shapeshifting{{AspectIcon|1}} ===
All Archkin can innately manipulate their forms using the [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Space|{{AspectIcon|1}}Space]] aspect. By shaping their body's [[Archkin#Archose|archose]] into various forms, they can take on a variety of '''shapes''', '''colors''', and '''sizes'''. Some do it to <u>express themselves</u>, others do it to hide.

This means <u>clothing</u> worn by Archkin are merely '''extensions''' of their body, woven from [[Archkin#Archose|archose]].

{{#SQuote:|text=Yes... that means all archkin are technically naked...<br />but don't call it that, they have nothing to hide anyways, it's FINE!|speaker=[[RAZEACC|Rayzie]]|receiver=[[You]]|attribution=|source=[[Archkin|while you read this page]]|id=W7RI-1RM0-612Z|template=}}

=== Psyche Planes ===
With enough [[Faith]], an Archkin can access and manipulate the '''pocket dimension''' within their own soul. Time does not pass normally in this plane, and it acts as a representative culmination of the archkin's personal experiences, [[Memories|memory]], and self. That is the '''Psyche Plane'''.<br>Normally, the plane is a '''barren white void''' with an invisible floor. But as one spends more time in it, the space '''alters''' the user's inner being.

This plane is typically used for deep thought and meditation, being a space free from the flow of time itself. However, as part of Archkin culture, this space is also often used for '''[[Psyche Duel|Psyche Duels]]'''.

When making physical contact with an Archkin, they may drag your [[PSYCHE (Soul)|soul]] into their Psyche Plane. Your [[PSYCHE (Soul)|soul]] would take on the form of your original body as you duel against the archkin.<br />
<u>Physical harm within a plane does '''not''' transfer into reality</u>, which is what made it perfect for '''[[Psyche Duel|Psyche Duels]]''' in Archkin Culture. Instead, once defeated, the loser would return to reality <u>completely exhausted</u> and with <u>blurry [[Memories|memories]]</u> for some time. This lack of long-lasting consequences is what made the culture thrive and prosper.
<br /><small>That is, unless you're dealing with [[Khueen|The Strongest Archgod]]. There is a reason few dared to challenge her, even fewer living to share what her Psyche Plane is like, and barely anyone knowing the ''truth'' of 'her plane'.</small>

== Biology and Physiology ==
As Archkin only get stronger from ''[[memories]]'' and [[Faith|''faith'']], that means they <u>don't need traditional sleep</u> and <u>food consumption</u> akin to mortals. They can still taste, but '''nothing conventional stops an Archkin from living indefinitely'''. However, they do still get fatigued when manipulating reality.

When in combat against people who are <u>unaware of an Archkin's weakness</u>, and are <u>trying to stay hidden</u> themselves, Archkin would often '''fake damage''' to avoid suspicion.<br />They'd shapeshift their archose to form <u>fake bruises</u>, spit out archose-molded <u>blood</u>, maybe even <u>overreact the pain</u>. Sometimes, if they're sloppy, you may even notice an Archkin <u>reacting to an attack a '''split second''' before actually feeling it</u>.<br />Once the coast is clear, they would just shapeshift back to their normal forms, and escape.

As for the traits of an Archkin's body, there are 4 distinct ones.

=== Frequency Shell ===
<blockquote>The most important part of an archkin's body. Due to their immense power, their true appearance is ''capable of shattering the fabric of space and time'' itself.<br /><small>Please refer to "[[The Fall of Marlika]]" for confirmation.</small>

As such, their frequency shell protects both '''themselves''' from <u>harm</u>, and '''us''' from <u>instant annihilation</u>. The frequency shell wraps around an entire archkin's body, encapsulating each and every [[Archkin#Archose|archose]] particle, similar to a thin bubble. It is invisible under normal circumstances.</blockquote>
=== Frequency Weakness ===
<blockquote>An Archkin's '''main weakness''' comes in the form of <u>frequencies</u>. When exposed to external stimuli that meddle with the delicate vibrations of their [[Archkin#Biology and Physiology#Frequency Shell|frequency shells]], it produces varying levels of '''discomfort'''.
These stimuli can range from <u>loud noises</u> at <u>high/low pitches</u>, <u>flames</u>, <u>electricity</u>, <u>music,</u> or [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Phantasy|phantasmal]] phenomena. But when under specifically '''2413Hz''' or '''3142Hz''', as felt in the [[Fracturing Sea]], the discomfort heightens to an '''electrifying'''/'''burning''' sensation that directly '''destabilizes their shell''' and seperates the bonds of their [[Archkin#Archose|archose]] <small>(hence why they're called the "Separatist" Frequencies)</small>, even to the point of risking the archkin's finality.</blockquote>

=== Intended Sterility ===
<blockquote>As Archkin were '''never invented for times of peace''', they were never designed with self-replication in-mind.

Archkin attempting to defy this decree by creating more Archkin is the <u>most severe act of defiance</u> against [[The Court of The 4th]].<br /><small>Please refer to "The Trial and Banishment of Archgod [[Khueen]] and Archgod [[Ouya]], and Archgod [[Kalayan]]"</small></blockquote>

=== Finality ===
<blockquote>The end of an Archkin is akin to a <u>star's supernova</u>.
<br />As an Archkin sustains damage, their [[Archkin#Biology and Physiology#Frequency Shell|frequency shell]] begins to crack, forming bright '''glitchy lights''' around the wound. These glitches are the <u>fabrics of space and time</u> physically '''bending''' due to the Archkin's true form leaking out.
Upon reaching finality, an archkin's [[Archkin#Biology and Physiology#Frequency Shell|shell]] implodes on itself in a final attempt to subdue as much of their innate power as possible. After the implosion, the remaining archose within the Archkin bursts out in an explosion of pure [[PSYCHE (Soul)|soul]] energy, burning and shredding anything nearby.
<br />Any disconnected [[Archkin#Archose|archose]] from that archkin would remain in the world, still containing its [[Memories|memories]], but without the ability for the archkin to control it.</blockquote>

== Archose ==
[[Category:Bestiary]]
<blockquote><small>''Etymology: (Archaic + Glucose = '''Archose''')''</small></blockquote>A dust-like particle that comprises all archkin's bodies. If [[PSYCHE (Soul)]] is known as "The Zenith Energy" to some, Archose is known as "The God Particle". Not only is '''ascended [[PSYCHE (Soul)|soul]] energy''', it also acts as the building blocks of matter itself.

Atoms, molecules, '''everything''' was once an archkin's archose. It merely adapts and changes over time as a living ''reprogrammable'' form of matter. This substance is how Archkin can manipulate [[The 4 Aspects of Reality]].<br />
Another trait of Archose is that it functions as '''all 5 senses'''. Archkin can <u>hear</u>, <u>smell</u>, <u>touch</u>, and <u>taste</u> from their Archose, with only archose dedicated to forming '''eyes''' being able to <u>see</u> out of.

=== Origins of Mana ===
In many universes, magical phenomena is a common feature. In many of those worlds, that arcane features can be attributed to a random Archkin who travelled that world long ago and left their archose behind (<small>a common pass-time of archkin back in [[The Archaic Aeons Era]])</small>.

When that archose mutates to the world it was left in, traces of that archkin's abilities manifest and festers into a different, but similar, phenomena. That is what many of these worlds call "'''Mana'''". People with <u>innate magical abilities</u> have '''trace doses of ancient archose''' imbued in their very [[PSYCHE (Soul)|soul]]. "Magical Phenomena" is the user utilizing their trace archose to manipulate the world.

== The Pandemonium State ==
<blockquote>As Archkin are innately connected to the very concept of [[PSYCHE (Soul)|souls]] and the [[memories]] within them, those building blocks can often be '''stimulated''' to a dangerous degree. 
When intense emotions flare up within an Archkin, the [[Memories|memory]] tied to those emotions can cause an <u>overwhelming increase in '''power'''</u> and <u>loss of '''control'''</u>. That is '''Pandemonium'''.<small>Please refer to "[[The Outpost 7 Catastrophe]]" and Archkin [[Archie]]'s hindered attempt at ascending to [[Archgod]]<nowiki/>hood.</small></blockquote>

==== How to deal with a Pandemonic Archkin? ====
<blockquote>Due to the average person's capabilities and resources, the <u>most common</u> course of action would be to '''escape''' or '''hide''' from the Archkin's attention as much as possible, especially when the flared-up emotion is anger. 
However, if a <u>capable archkin</u> is present, or with the right equipment, it may be best to '''face''' the pandemonic archkin in a '''[[Psyche Duel]]''' to tire them out near-instantly. But do be prepared, as you would be facing the eye of the storm, a pandemonic archkin is even more powerful during a '''[[Psyche Duel]]'''.</blockquote>

{{Faction_Template|title1=The Stone Society|type=Stone-Age Civilization|status=Active|era=[[The Spirepocalypse Era]]|hub=[["The Continent"#Newpoint Village|Newpoint Village]]|goal=- Bring back Modernity and everyone's memories<br>

The '''main playable faction''' in the [[Firmata Frontier]] roleplay [[Projects|project]].

The Stone Society is a collection of many different sentient species and races. However, their main two groups consist of the [["Locals"]] who were memory-wiped and [["Otherworlders"]] who were taken away from their homeworlds. 

Although their tech is still mostly in the <u>Neolithic stage</u>, they've managed to climb up and skip through several early scientific advancements due to the efforts of [[Professor Besado]] in the past 20 years since [[The Spirepocalypse]]. And now, with [["Otherworlders"]] joining in and sharing their knowledge, science is bound to advance at a much faster pace. '''Basic farming''' is already being slowly discovered through [[Darren Holt]]'s efforts, proper '''training regiments''' for both Locals and Otherworlders have been expertly conducted by [[Therion]], '''architectural advancements''' are being made through the fractured remnants of [[Altarhea]]'s previous memories and experiences <small>(some of these can be seen in Newpoint Village)</small>, while '''investigations''' into the mysteries surrounding these isolated lands are being spearheaded by Detective [[Kafka Cervantes]] and his apparent assistant [[Viktor]].

Currently, their technology is somewhere in between '''Neolithic''' and '''Medieval'''. And with the recent completion of the Hot Air Balloon project, advancement has begun to spread out once. A new outpost is currently in the works, villages are being reinforced, and new/more resources are being collected.

These survivors still mostly rely on '''foraging''' and '''hunting''' for food. '''Flint tools''' are beginning to replace their original stone tools, and simple metallurgy is being worked on thanks to the discovery of [[Wynaquet]].

Things are looking up, and advancements are well under way.

= Overview =
A secret organization with very few members compared to other factions across the infinite fields. Despite that, The Vicars of Null have a '''disastrously-notorious influence''' and presence across the lands. Some vicars are capable of facing off against [[Archgod|Archgods]], while others can turn whole civilizations against themselves.

They are a rag-tag group of bandits who works as agents of [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Phantasy|Phantasy]], with the sole purpose of disrupting [[The Court of The 4th|our]] operations in the fictional plane.

== Tech and Capabilities ==
Due to the wide variety of vicars in this syndicate, it is difficult to formulate an accurate assessment of their overall technology and prowess. However, there are a few notable traits they all share.

Vicars seem to possess equipment, or abilities, that are highly-attuned to '''manipulating [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Fracturing|Fracturing]] and [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#NulGraven|NulGraven]]'''. This allows them to create "zones" or "sites" at seemingly any time. However, despite their allegiance, it seems the [[caradel]] from these fabrications remain hostile even to them.

A vast majority of Vicars share some form of '''immortality''', whether that be through their bodies being reformed on "death", or simply refusing to age. Their leader seems to favour bringing in the long life spanned into their fold.

Although it's still possible for us to do so, '''Vicars have a form of resistance to [[You|your]] ability to manipulate fate'''. It is currently unknown how they possess such a trait.

= Organization =

== The "Innocent" Prince ==
[[File:NulDetta.png|left|thumb]]
Their masked and unknown leader, an amalgamative hivemind of every single soul that has ceased to exist. 

<br>Also known by the [[Vicars of Null#Vicars|vicars]] as "''The Innocent Prince''", for every character you wrote whose parents were left unwritten, they have joined The Prince.<br>Every character concept you ignored, thrown away, or forgotten. The Prince has accepted them.<br>Characters from stories left unfinished or cancelled, they persist within The Prince.<br>When entire worlds of fiction are overwritten or ended by [[You]], they've become one with The Null. 

As the embodiment of [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Phantasy|Phantasy]] itself, The Prince acts as the antithesis to [[The Court of The 4th]] on their own. It is with utmost urgency and importance that any hint of The "Innocent" Prince's activities are reported directly to court staff, or at the very least, any active Fourth Marshals. 

Although they exist solely within [[The Null Crossing]], their influence and manipulations manage to bleed into the infinite fields. If this activity isn't due to their vicars, it's brought about by their [[The Vicars of Null#The Flower Girl and The Ring Bearer|kin]].

== The Flower Girl and [[Dett|The Ring Bearer]] ==
[[File:FlowerGirlAndRingBearer.png|right|frameless|268x268px]]

Every <u>child</u> that has forgotten how to live— rests within '''[[Dett|The Ring Bearer]]'''.<br>Every <u>teenager</u> robbed of their freedom— rebels as '''The Flower Girl'''.

These two entities act as the direct influences of [[The Vicars of Null#The Prince|The Prince]] into the infinite fields, they are [[The Vicars of Null#The Prince|The Prince]]'s kin.<br>'''[[Dett|The Ring Bearer]]''' merely <u>scouts</u> out and <u>experiences life</u> as an emissary or spy. ''He'' is the '''harbinger''' of {{Phantasy}} Phantasy's [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Fracturing|'''Fracturing''']].<br>'''The Flower Girl''' acts as a <u>warden</u> for the vicars, eternally watching through her echoes. ''She'' '''embodies''' {{Phantasy}} Phantasy's [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#NulGraven|'''NulGraven''']].<br>Together, they bridge the gap between a [[Ontological Class|Beyond]] and Fictional.

Due to them being constructs of [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Phantasy|Phantasy]], they must wear ''special wrappings'' at all times. They may have their wrappings appear human-like, using similar shapeshifting abilities to [[Archkin]]. But if their antimatter-like skin is ever exposed to existence, they would be subjected to indescribable pain— while also risking the fabrication of [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Entropy|Entropic]] [[Caradels]] in the nearby area.<br>It is for both their, and [[You|your]] best interest to '''avoid fighting either one of these kin and fabrications''' as much as possible. Although they're not [[Archgods]], their capabilities are <u>on-par</u> with them, often even exceling most when it comes to manipulating Phantasy.

== Vicars ==
[[File:VicarMembers.png|left|frameless|420x420px]]

These are the '''main workforce''' of this organization, the super soldier agents of [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Phantasy|Phantasy]] constructed from flesh, bone, metal, or dust. They span across several eras, through countless areas. And no matter where they go, what they have to do, or what lives they're living— their sole purpose is always to oppose [[You]]<nowiki/>r will.

There are instances of [[The Vicars of Null#Vicars|vicars]] attacking characters following a writer merely planning something that [[The Vicars of Null#The Prince|The Prince]] saw as a "potential deviation from [[The Vicars of Null#The True Formula|The True Formula]]". Other [[The Vicars of Null#Vicars|vicars]] were spotted messing with supply chains or infrastructure in cities, forcing writers to reconsider their plans due to the continuation of them now causing "plot holes".<br>Despite their frequent instances of "meta-gaming" and breaking The 4th's Wall, they carry average levels of Lucidity. They are merely <u>given instructions by The Prince</u>, '''they cannot see through fiction''' under normal circumstances. 

Each [[The Vicars of Null#Vicars|Vicar]] wears a [[The Vicars of Null#Vicar Masks|vicar mask]]. Each one carries a dream. And each one carries a "calculation", one foretold by [[The Vicars of Null#The Prince|The Prince]].<br>It is up to them to decide whether a [[The Vicars of Null#Vicars|vicar]] is ready to learn the truth of their world.

=== Honorary Vicars ===
On rare instances, someone may be given the title of an "Honorary Vicar". This is often a temporary role given to civilians during dire situations within [[The Vicars of Null#Vicars|Vicar]] activities.<br>After being given their temporary [[The Vicars of Null#Vicar Masks|Vicar Mask]], they're given a fraction of the privileges all [[The Vicars of Null#Vicars|Vicars]] share, such as opening "zones" or "sites" and being able to commune directly with [[The Vicars of Null#The Prince|The Prince]].

If the situation isn't critical, however, this title may also be given to people with fleeting roles in the organization. From '''mask makers''', to people "'''babysitting'''" [[Dett|The Ring Bearer]].
----

=== Believers ===
Individuals exposed to the influences of vicar activities, or finding themselves lost in deep [[The 4 Aspects of Reality#Entropy|entropy]], may find themselves to whisperings from a Beyond— The Prince. They scramble the [[The Vicars of Null#Writing the Number of Phantasy|Number of Phantasy]] over and over again in a ritualistic manner, trying their best to strengthen and continue their connection to [[The Vicars of Null#The Prince|The Prince]] as much as possible. If not that, they endlessly scribble and write stories constantly to envision themselves in better places, in better times.

These are not mass gatherings of followers or grand cathedrals to a higher being. But rather, isolated churches made from singled-out lonely victims of solitude— people who are desperate to feel some form of connection from anything, even if that something was more fictional than they were.

= Overview =
Composed of all humans beyond [[The 4th's Wall]], whether they're aware of it or not, '''The 4th Beyond''' are the scribes of fiction's fate. They are metaphysical beings that rest on a plane higher than the characters of these tales, each member has their own reason for meddling with the world of fantasy— from mere entertainment or self-fulfilment, or in search for a greater purpose or to push a deeper message.

As a member of The 4th Beyond, it is nigh critical for [[you]] to recognize your place in the fictional world, sooner or later. Acknowledge your capabilities with it, your influence beyond ontological scale, and the role you play in the narrative.

= Tech and Capabilities =

== Skribbles ==
[[File:SkribbleArt.png|left|thumb]]
Making up a vast majority of The Court's inventory, '''skribbles''' are the <u>primordial flickers of creativity</u>, birthed when humanity first dabbled with the concept of fiction, long before pens were around.

Manifesting as soul flickers of pale green flames and often taking on spirit-like appearances, these little fatelings are what you use to script the fate of characters in your stories.<br>When something bad happens to a character because you wrote it to happen, you wrote that thanks to your skribble. As the main character pushes forward against all odds, they persist due to your insistence, through your skribble. They are the '''bridge''' between your <u>creative will</u>, and their <u>fictional reality</u>.

However, due to the anciency of this concept, skribbles have gone through many reiterations, revisions, and improvements over the years.<br>Long ago, they were <u>a lot more creative</u> and carried much more <u>free will</u>, as humanity has yet to fully grasp the concept of creativity and what limits it lacks. These manifested in wild but authentic cave paintings glittered atop a firmament of stone, backlit merely by humanity's own flickering flame.<br>As society advanced, so did the skribbles.  

When hieroglyphics were being carved, their individual will expressed as unique iconography for every character in ancient human scripts. As pens were born, skribbles grew tails on their necks, mimicking our own quill tipped creations. However, once the printing press was invented, problems began to arise.  

Their individuality and free will started becoming a problem. Although the population of scribbles grew exponentially in relation to the number of creative humans in your world, that also carried a much wilder and less comprehensive mass of creativity. Letters were harder to print, ideas more difficult to comprehend. Then, this climaxed with the [[Farewell Avalon|Protos and Archie Incident]].  

Ever since then, Skribbles have been <u>mostly neutered of their own free will</u>, giving more focus to you writers instead.<br>Their right arms have been severed, fully symbolizing their position as your "left-hand men" in writing stories. Their voice, taken away and replaced with your own. Creativity, individuality, all depended on their owners. And although you may never feel them, they are always by your side.<br>Though they no longer have a voice, some believe that your "booms of inspiration" are the whispers of your skribble, doing its best to break through its silence to bring you back to creativity.  
== Skravatars ==
Although they carry similarities to Skribbles, Skravatars complete an entirely different function and purpose. Rather than acting as your pen to script fate, <u>skravatars carry your will into fiction</u>. Essentially, they are '''self-insert''' characters.

This is not to be mistaken with the typical fictional trope of having a bland and empty main character for the viewer/reader/player to project themselves. Instead, skravatars are '''entirely blank slates''', who's <u>appearance</u>, <u>backstory</u>, and <u>personality</u> are '''yours''' and entirely your own. They carry no free will whatsoever and are solely meant for you to interact with the fictional world yourself, without needing to use a skribble to puppeteer a character.

Although skravatars are mostly reserved for Fourth Marshal members, there are no requirements to you writing your own skravatar. While it is suggested they carry pale green color motifs to match with The Court, this is not a requirement either.

== The World Builder ==
She is your right-hand man in wiring stories. If Skribbles control the narrative, and Skravatars lets you playtest it, ''she'' lets you build the setting.

Just like skribbles, you use her whenever you build a new world. Although, direct visitations are strictly off-limits.
