## 


> Also known as The Spireknell following The Spirepocalypse, he was the lead lab assistant of Morgale's Living Piece in An-Tri'fhiana.
> Initially a caretaker in Marlika, he joined Avalon seeking greater purpose. He originally served under Morgale herself, but was eventually transitioned to her Living Piece after Morgale had to continue the exodus, leaving her Living Piece and Lithe on Lejos to continue working on their soul experiements. ### In truth... (Full Intel)
> A failed experimental Caradeled Archkin, made in the hopes of "upgrading" all Avalonian Archkin to be compatible with Morgale's 2413Hz archose and cariburn archamachines, Lithe was made the direct lab assistant of Morgale's Living Piece, being her personal "magnum opus". Prior to The Spirepocalypse, he was designated as the only person "spared" from the memory-wipe, which Morgale's Living Piece did to creare HER Firmata. His mission following The Spirepocalypse was to slowly rebuild and strengthen An-Tri'fhiana by taking advantage of the considerably faster-flowing time within HER Firmata (20 years in The World's Aplenty is estimated to have been between 20 decades to 20 centuries within HER Firmata) to extract enough "core memories" to revive as many Avalonians as possible— a near-identical mission given to Professor Besado upon her birth.
> Following The Spirepocalypse, Lithe took on the mantle of The Spireknell, a memory-stealing fiend who systematically hunted down anyone who somehow broke into HER Firmata and circumvented the Memory Wipe upon entry (all people, outside of Otherworlders, entering have their memories wiped again). This is to avoid having to rely on feasting upon Morgale's Failed Epicenter, which contains all the stolen memories used to keep HER Firmata active. Lithe only feasts on the stolen memories if absolutely necessary.
> With the Survivors of Avalon mostly revived, he's currently working on trying to find notes from Morgale and her piece based on a smaller research project they ran on Beyonders. Not only is he aware of The 4th Beyond, he's actively seeking them out. Although he can't see nor sense them due to a low Lucidity, he's trying to get them to "rewrite" or "retcon" certain mechanics with Failed Epicenters to at least "free" Morgale's Living Piece in some way, to have a normal form again. He's willing to pay whatever price that'll be, whatever it takes.

## Personality
> On the outside, she’s very bubbly and positive, yapping about science and fun facts to keep people’s moods up while teaching things in an exciting manner.
> 
> But alone, Besado gets highly calculative and proficient with her work, taking on every project she’s the highest level of seriousness and dedication possible. Although a bit of an overworker, with the fate of society on her shoulders, she feels there’s no other way out.

## Appearance
> She wears a simple long-sleeved woven top made from hemp cloth and dyed with charcoal powder. The formula “A2 + B2 = C2” can be seen written near her collar using dried blood— the Pythagorean theorem. Her pants are also made of hemp cloth, just like all the other fabrics made and used by the villagers. But unlike her top, this remains undyed.
> 
> A belt is strawn across her ways, carrying a pouch for soft carryables and a small tanned anime-hide pocket for sharper collectibles. More hemp cloth is tied around her feet to act as socks, with simple wooden paddings serving as shoes.
> 
> Aside from clothing, her pure-white hair drapes down to the back of her thighs, it seems she hasn’t had time to trim it. And accompanying her soft round face, is a pair of inquisitive and glittering yellow eyes, always gazing upon the world in deep wonder.

## Goals
> On the outside, she wishes to rebuild society to what it once was, and to uncover the mysteries of both The Spirepocalypse and The Otherworlders’ Arrivals. (In truth, she wants to set the world up for "eternal piece" from the Archgod Revoluion, so she can eventually end herself, as she's aware of the Besado lineage being the founders of the AoS and beginning the war against Mortals and Archkin... the guilt of this has been eating her away).

## Specialties
### Math Prodigy
> Besado excels in mathematics. In fact, it’s her greatest skill, being able to act as a human calculator with ease. It’s her greatest source of internal joy.
### Science Lover
> Her love for the sciences is more easily shared. It’s her main method of encouragement while uplifting lives, making it her greatest source of external joy.
### Forced Will
> Due to how much pressure she puts on herself, Besado’s will is stronger than most. Tempered through intense mental anguish, she can brave through many storms most would falter over.
> However, without someone to confide in, even she is approaching her limit.

## Vulnerabilities
### Chemistry
> Despite her scientific knowledge, Besado is notoriously bad when it comes to mixing chemicals.
### Weak Physicality
> During her upbringing, the villagers mostly tended to all the menial labor while following her orders for projects. Although it made progress faster as she focused more on managing, she grew up with very little physical experience.
> (In truth: This was due to Besado having Memetta do all the menial work while for her during the Long Shush, choosing to do barely any manual labor herself. And since Memetta can just... bestow life onto rocks, there was no reason for her to do anything.)
### Overbearing Mentality
> Besado puts too much pressure on herself and feels greatly justified for doing so. This is made worse by her not having anyone close enough to confide in, as she fears her worries may spread to the rest of her survivors, lowering morale. She is approaching a breaking point.

## Abilities

Besado has an unusual amount of knowledge from the modern age, helping her at least maintain the stone-age society up until the arrival of The Otherworlders.
She can keep up in conversations with them, but only up to a point. Whatever that point is, it’s pretty inconsistent.
