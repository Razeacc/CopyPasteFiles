# Story Summary
## Before HER
There was no Infinite Fields.
No Worlds Aplenty.
No Epicenters.
No Archkin.
Not even time itself has properly been invented or organized, causing any sustained injuries and lessons learned from training to grow and adapt at wildly-inconsistent rates.
Reality was effectively operating inside what would later become known as the Null Crossing era.
A much more primitive metaphysical landscape.

Here, Archgods waged war against Phantycians over the right to create, the right to exist. Fueled and goaded on by The Fourth Beyond, we commanded the Archgods to live and die at war for our entertainment. There was no true purpose or goal for this struggle, merely a wish to see characters strive against conflict and push through it. Times were simpler, fiction was easier to swallow, and the goals of Archgods were black and white.

## The Slacking Archgod
### Ulhysse the Human
> NOTE: This section is mostly top-secret. The Origin and Truth of Ulhysse being originally a "real" person from our world is meant to be a massive spoiler and plot twist for the whole story. This is because once Ulhysse's true backstory as a real person is revealed to Tool, she's banished by The Fourth Beyond and taken back into our world to properly move on into our afterlife. Her backstory is a cognito hazard against herself.
Originally being a normal human girl living somewhere in the Mediterranian during the distant past, Ulhysse was named as such due to her parents being fans of the stories of Ulysses. Over time, Ulhysse grew distant with life, finding the struggle of reality being harder to keep up with as days passed. Only the comfort of fiction kept her going. This progressed to the point that she wished to become fictional instead.
Eventually, she passed away at a young age. However, her soul remained in a limbo state. She remained as such, until someone accidentally wrote a story about Ulysses, but mispelling their tale's character to unintentionally detail herself instead: Ulhysse.

### Ulhysse the Archgod
After being mispelled as a new archgod similar to Morgale, Ulhysse was brought into the Null Crossing just like any other archgod. Kalayan came after, being a typo of the word "Kalayaan" instead. It seemed a recent wave of typos went through the cracks, as Morgale (a typo of Morgan) was also fabricated shortly after.

While most archgods were created with their purpose instilled into them (train to fight Phantycians), Ulhysse had none of this, due to her technically being a reincarnated member of The Fourth Beyond. Her innate authority overwritten whatever was instilled into archgods, causing her to instead laze around all day playing music on her Archgod Sitar.
The Archgod Sitar was this stringless instrument. To play it, Archkin would shapeshift their bodies to create strings on the frettboard. From there, they'd manually tightne and loosen their shapeshifted strings to make whatever sound they wanted. 

Using this instrument, Ulhysse often bothered Kalayan as he trained under Captain Gilgamesh. Her bothering and annoyanced progressed to the point where Kalayan was forced to wear a mask that obscured his true expression in hopes of warding off Ulhysse.
It did not work.

## Pandemonium
Some time has passed with Ulhysse constantly pestering and annoying Kalayan during his training. Despite that, they've both grown up quite well.
With Archgod Gilgamesh falling in battle, Beowulf took the mantle, with Kalayan taking over his place as well as one of the Captains of the Archgod.

Ulhysse, meanwhile, has devoted and expanded her craft from not just merely music. She's began dabbling in the art of emotions too. She wondered why, when she felt certain ways, she felt herself get weaker or stronger. Kalayan noticed this too, and it's what he utilized to rise up the ranks the way he did, knowing when to use his anger, and when sadness was required for defense.

This is, essentially, a micro-analyzation of the Pandemonium State.
While most people in the modern day know very mad = outraged archkin, Ulhysse noticed that ANY emotion tweaked and fine-tuned an Archkin's prowess to the nth degree. Although Pandemonium itself wasn't discovered yet, Ulhysse (and maybe Khueen) were the only archkin alive to have not only noticed this fundamental truth about Archkin/Archgod physiology, but fully understood it as well.
This was Kalayan's only leg up to other Archgods, as there was 1 core problem everyone faced: Damage was near-permanent. Since time hasn't been invented yet, lots of effects normally attributed to time were somewhat... off.. Yes, people can still grow and learn, but they can't heal over time.
It's very messy and inconsistent.

However, that all changed one day.

## Birth of The Creator
Kalayan, Baeowulf, Morgan, Morgale, Ulysses, Ulhysse, along with a BUNCH of other archgods, were all moving along to a spot near the main frontline of the war.
There were rumors of it being a new safe haven in The Null Crossing, so they were hoping to confirm its safety and quickly migrate a large group there to secure it. Although these rumors came from a little boy and girl, they felt safe enough to trust.
From experience, all Phantycians were tall 4-armed masked monstrous beings. But these two kids, although didn't follow archkin protocol like Ulhysse, were seemingly harmless. Ulhysse' harmless nature somewhat told the Archgods that this was possible.
The little boy, named Dett, led the group to this safe haven. While the teenage girl, Memetta, watched their back. They claimed to be archkin, but just like Ulhysse, they too were peaceful.

As the migrational exodus seemed to drag on for a bit too long, Kalayan was the first to notice... something was approaching them. But as he turned around, Dett's form shifted and twisted, turning into a Phantycian as well: Nul Detta's primal form. Unbeknownst to the archkin, Phantycians were capable of shapeshifting this whole time, too. And Nul kept that secret in their arsenal for this exact moment. In less than a second, Entropic Cradles erupted from Dett, instantly killing Kalayan, Beowulf, and Morgan. Meanwhile, Memetta shapeshifted into her true form as well, deleting Ulysses, severely injuring Morgale, and barely missing Ulhysse. The two Phantycians proceeded to tear apart many of the other Archgods in the vicinity as Ulhysse ran away.
In less than a second, her closest friends and family were all gone.
